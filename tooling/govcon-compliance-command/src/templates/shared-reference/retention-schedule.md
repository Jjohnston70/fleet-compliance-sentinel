# Universal Retention Schedule
**Purpose:** Master retention schedule for all record types across all organizational contexts
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-07
**Authority:** data-handling-privacy skill (Records Management Policy)
**Scope:** All records regardless of format (paper, electronic, audio, video) or location

---

## Overview

This retention schedule establishes the baseline retention periods for all organizational records. It is extracted from data-handling-privacy skill Template 07 (Records Management Policy) and serves as the authoritative source for retention requirements across all compliance skills.

### Universal Retention Principles

1. **Data-as-Regulated:** All records treated as regulated by default; retention ensures compliance
2. **Baseline + Overlay:** This schedule provides baseline retention; federal, state, or client requirements may override
3. **Legal Hold Precedence:** Legal holds suspend normal disposition until released
4. **Most Stringent Applies:** When multiple retention requirements conflict, longest period applies
5. **Secure Disposition:** All records disposed of securely per classification level

### Authority Hierarchy

- **Baseline Authority:** data-handling-privacy skill (Records Management Policy)
- **Federal Overlay:** government-contracting skill (federal retention may be longer)
- **State Overlay:** State laws may require longer retention (e.g., employment records)
- **Client Overlay:** Client contracts may require longer retention
- **Industry Overlay:** Industry regulations may require longer retention (HIPAA, SOX, IRS)

## Legal Hold Procedures

### Legal Hold Identification

Legal holds are identified by Legal Counsel for:
- Actual or anticipated litigation
- Government investigation or inquiry
- Regulatory examination or audit
- Internal investigation requiring evidence preservation

### Legal Hold Implementation

When legal hold is issued:
1. **Automated disposition suspended** for all records within scope
2. **Records preserved in place** or copied to secure, immutable location
3. **Custodians notified** and acknowledge understanding
4. **Legal hold tracking system** maintains status

### Legal Hold Scope

Legal hold notice includes:
- **Custodians:** Individuals holding relevant records
- **Date range:** Time period of records covered
- **Subject matter:** Description of records covered
- **Duration:** Until released by Legal Counsel

### Legal Hold Release

When legal matter concludes:
1. **Legal Counsel authorizes release**
2. **Release notice sent to custodians**
3. **Normal retention schedules resume**
4. **Records eligible for disposition are reviewed**

## Retention Period Determination Guidelines

### How to Set Retention Periods

When determining retention for record types not listed:

**Step 1: Identify Legal Requirements**
- Federal laws (IRS, DOL, EEOC, SEC, etc.)
- State laws (employment, privacy, tax, etc.)
- Industry regulations (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, FERPA, GLBA, etc.)
- Contractual obligations

**Step 2: Identify Business Needs**
- Operational use (how long actively needed?)
- Historical reference (future use likely?)
- Legal defense (statute of limitations)
- Audit requirements

**Step 3: Apply Longest Requirement**
- Compare legal, regulatory, contractual, and business needs
- Apply longest retention period
- Document rationale

**Step 4: Add Buffer**
- Consider adding 1-2 years to statutory minimum for safety
- Account for delayed discovery or audit initiation

### Common Retention Periods by Legal Basis

| Legal Basis | Typical Retention | Rationale |
|-------------|------------------|-----------|
| IRS tax audit period | 3 years (6 years if substantial understatement) | IRS assessment period |
| Statute of limitations (contracts) | 6 years (written), 4 years (oral) in most states | State law varies |
| Employment law (FLSA) | 3 years (payroll), 2 years (wage records) | Federal minimum |
| Employment law (EEOC) | 1 year | Federal minimum; states may require longer |
| HIPAA | 6 years | Regulatory minimum |
| ERISA | 6 years | Regulatory minimum |
| SOX (audit workpapers) | 7 years | Public company requirement |
| FAR (federal contracts) | 3 years after final payment | Federal minimum |

**Organization Standard:** 7 years for most business and financial records provides comprehensive coverage

## Disposition Procedures

### Disposition Triggers

Records are disposed when:
- Retention period expires
- Legal hold is released
- Business need no longer exists
- Superseded by newer version (for policies/procedures)

### Disposition Authorization

Before disposition:
1. **Verify retention period complete**
2. **Check for legal holds** (none active)
3. **Obtain approval** (Records Management Officer or designee)
4. **Document disposition** (what, when, how, by whom)

### Disposition Methods by Media Type

| Media Type | Disposition Method | Standard |
|------------|-------------------|----------|
| Paper records (non-confidential) | Cross-cut shredding | NIST SP 800-88 |
| Paper records (confidential/restricted) | Cross-cut shredding, certificate of destruction | NIST SP 800-88 |
| Electronic media (hard drives, SSDs) | Cryptographic erasure or physical destruction | NIST SP 800-88 Rev 1 |
| Removable media (USB, external drives) | Cryptographic erasure or physical destruction | NIST SP 800-88 |
| Cloud storage | Secure deletion per platform procedures | Google Cloud data deletion |
| Backup tapes | Degaussing or physical destruction | NIST SP 800-88 |
| Mobile devices | Factory reset + MDM wipe + physical destruction | NIST SP 800-88 |

### Disposition Documentation

For each disposition event, document:
- Record type and description
- Retention period applied
- Disposition date
- Disposition method
- Authorization (who approved)
- Certificate of destruction (if applicable)

## Federal Overlay: FAR Retention Requirements

Federal contracts are subject to Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) retention requirements:

**FAR 4.703: Policy**
- **Minimum Retention:** 3 years after final payment
- **Applies To:** Contract files, supporting documents, related correspondence

**Organization Policy:**
- Organization uses **7 years** for all contracts (federal and commercial) for consistency
- 7 years exceeds FAR minimum and aligns with statute of limitations

**Federal Records Covered:**
- Contract award documents
- Statements of Work (SOW)
- Modifications and change orders
- Invoices and payment records
- Deliverables and acceptance documentation
- Correspondence with Contracting Officer
- Performance reports
- Closeout documentation

**Rationale:**
- Statute of limitations (Contract Disputes Act): 6 years
- False Claims Act: 6 years from violation or 3 years from discovery
- Organization uses 7 years to exceed all federal requirements

See government-contracting skill for detailed federal retention procedures.
