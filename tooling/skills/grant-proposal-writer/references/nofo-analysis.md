## Federal NOFO Analysis {#federal}

Federal NOFOs follow a predictable structure. Knowing where to look saves time.

### Key Sections to Extract From

**Section I: Program Description**
- Funding purpose and priorities
- Eligible activities and populations
- Evidence-based practice requirements
- Required program components or models
- Any mandatory partnerships or collaborations

**Section II: Award Information**
- Funding amount (ceiling, floor)
- Number of awards expected
- Project period (years)
- Cost-sharing or match requirements
- Funding mechanism (grant, cooperative agreement)

**Section III: Eligibility**
- Eligible applicant types (nonprofit, government, tribal, etc.)
- Geographic restrictions
- Required registrations (SAM.gov, UEI)
- Any priority populations or areas
- Disqualifying factors

**Section IV: Application and Submission**
- Required sections and their order
- Page limits (per section and total)
- Format requirements (font, margins, spacing)
- Required forms (SF-424 family)
- Required attachments
- Submission method and deadline

**Section V: Review Criteria**
- THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT SECTION
- Scoring criteria with point allocations
- What reviewers are told to look for in each criterion
- Any bonus points or priority areas
- This is literally the rubric -- write to it

**Section VI: Administration**
- Reporting requirements
- Terms and conditions
- Monitoring expectations

### SAMHSA-Specific NOFO Elements
- Required GPRA data collection
- National Outcome Measures (NOMs)
- Evidence-based practice registry references
- Cultural competence requirements
- Health disparities language
- Connection to 988 Lifeline (if crisis-related)
- Specific population priority areas

### VA-Specific NOFO Elements
- Veteran eligibility verification requirements
- Coordination with VA Medical Centers
- VA-specific reporting tools and timelines
- Alignment with VA strategic priorities
- Definition of "veteran" per 38 U.S.C.

### DOL-Specific NOFO Elements
- WIOA alignment requirements
- Common Measures (entered employment, retention, earnings)
- Employer engagement expectations
- Workforce development board coordination

## State/Local RFP Analysis {#state}

State RFPs are less standardized than federal NOFOs but follow general patterns.

### Colorado CDHS RFPs
- Check alignment with CDHS strategic plan
- Look for OBH (Office of Behavioral Health) specific requirements
- Note Colorado crisis services integration expectations
- Identify state reporting system requirements
- Check for Colorado-specific data requirements

### What to Extract
- Eligible applicant types and geographic scope
- Program alignment requirements
- Budget constraints and allowable costs
- Match or cost-share requirements
- Reporting frequency and format
- Evaluation expectations
- Required attachments and certifications
- Whether fiscal sponsors are accepted

## Community Foundation Analysis {#foundation}

Community foundations are less formal but have clear preferences.

### El Pomar Foundation
- Colorado-only funder based in Colorado Springs
- Review current funding priorities on their website
- Note any specific program areas they've highlighted
- Check grant size ranges for the specific program
- Shorter narrative expected (typically 3-10 pages)
- Value organizational stability and track record
- Want to see community impact, not just program activities
- Board list with affiliations required
- Audited financials usually required for larger requests

### Pikes Peak Community Foundation
- El Paso and Teller County focus
- Check current competitive grant cycle timeline
- Note specific focus areas for the current cycle
- Collaboration with other local orgs is highly valued
- Want to see the applicant knows the local landscape
- Practical outcomes over theoretical frameworks

### General Foundation Analysis
- What has this funder funded before? (check their 990 or annual report)
- What language do they use to describe their priorities?
- Do they have a strategic plan or theory of change?
- How formal or informal is their application process?
- Do they accept LOIs (letters of inquiry) first?
- What's their typical grant size and duration?
- Do they fund new organizations or require a track record?

## Analysis Output Template {#template}

After analyzing the NOFO/RFP, create this summary before writing:

```
NOFO ANALYSIS SUMMARY
=====================

FUNDER: [Name]
OPPORTUNITY: [Title and Number]
DEADLINE: [Date] — [X days from today]
AMOUNT: [Range or Fixed]
PERIOD: [X years]
TYPE: [Federal / State / Foundation]

ELIGIBILITY CONFIRMED: [Yes / No / Need to verify: ___]

REQUIRED SECTIONS (in NOFO order):
1. [Section name] — [Max pages/words] — [Points: XX]
2. [Section name] — [Max pages/words] — [Points: XX]
3. ...

EVALUATION CRITERIA (by weight):
1. [Criterion] — [XX points] — [What reviewers look for]
2. [Criterion] — [XX points] — [What reviewers look for]
3. ...

REQUIRED ATTACHMENTS:
- [ ] [Attachment name]
- [ ] [Attachment name]
- ...

REQUIRED FORMS:
- [ ] [Form name]
- ...

FORMAT REQUIREMENTS:
- Font: [Specified or not]
- Margins: [Specified or not]
- Spacing: [Specified or not]
- File format: [PDF / Word / Online portal]

BUDGET CONSTRAINTS:
- Match required: [Yes (X%) / No]
- Indirect cost limit: [X% or NICRA accepted]
- Unallowable costs: [List any mentioned]
- Cost categories required: [List]

KEY FUNDER LANGUAGE TO MIRROR:
- [Term or phrase the NOFO uses repeatedly]
- [Term or phrase the NOFO uses repeatedly]
- ...

PROGRAM FIT NOTES:
- [How the proposed program aligns with funder priorities]
- [Any gaps between program and funder expectations]
- [Required adaptations to program description]

INFORMATION GAPS (need from user):
- [Data or content needed to complete the proposal]
- ...
```
