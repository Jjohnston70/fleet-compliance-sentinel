## Colorado State Grants {#colorado-state}

### Colorado Department of Human Services (CDHS)
- Must demonstrate alignment with CDHS strategic priorities
- Must describe target population with Colorado-specific data
- Must reference Colorado behavioral health transformation efforts (if mental health related)
- Must describe coordination with existing CDHS-funded services in the region
- Check: Does the proposal use Colorado data (not just national statistics)?

### Office of Behavioral Health (OBH)
- Must align with Colorado's Behavioral Health Administration (BHA) priorities
- Must describe integration with Colorado Crisis Services (if crisis-related)
- Must address access for rural and frontier communities if statewide
- Must describe how services complement existing behavioral health safety net
- Check: Does the proposal reference Colorado crisis infrastructure?

### Colorado Health Foundation and Similar State Funders
- Often require community engagement evidence
- Health equity lens required
- Must demonstrate cultural responsiveness to Colorado demographics
- Geographic focus areas may be specified
- Check: Does the proposal address health equity and community engagement?

## Community Foundations {#community-foundations}

### El Pomar Foundation
- Colorado-only funder (Colorado Springs headquarters)
- Prioritizes organizations demonstrating community impact in Colorado
- Typically funds general operating, program support, and capital projects
- Grant range: $10,000 - $100,000+ depending on program
- Requires clear demonstration of need within the specific Colorado community served
- Values organizational sustainability and track record
- Check: Is the proposal specific to a Colorado community? Does it demonstrate local impact?

**Common El Pomar Requirements:**
- Organization must be based in Colorado
- Must be a 501(c)(3) or government entity (or fiscal sponsor arrangement)
- Must provide most recent audited financial statements
- Must include board list with affiliations
- Must describe how grant funds specifically will be used
- Narrative should be concise and outcome-focused

### Pikes Peak Community Foundation
- Serves El Paso and Teller County communities
- Competitive grants typically in $10,000 - $50,000 range
- Focus areas: education, human services, community development
- Values collaboration and avoiding duplication of services
- Check: Does the proposal demonstrate knowledge of the Pikes Peak region specifically?

**Common Pikes Peak CF Requirements:**
- Must serve El Paso or Teller County
- Must demonstrate collaboration with other local organizations
- Must provide program-specific budget (not just org budget)
- Must describe measurable outcomes
- Must show awareness of existing services and how proposed program fills a gap

### General Community Foundation Requirements
Community foundations generally look for:
- Local data supporting the need (not just national statistics)
- Evidence of community input in program design
- Partnerships with other local organizations
- Realistic and specific outcomes
- Clear budget tied to proposed activities
- Organizational capacity to deliver
- Plans for sustainability beyond the grant period
- Board governance and financial health

**Common Community Foundation Red Flags:**
- Using only national statistics without local data
- No evidence of community input or engagement
- Duplicating existing services without acknowledging them
- Unrealistic outcomes for the budget requested
- No plan for what happens when grant funding ends
- Proposal reads like a federal grant application (too formal, too much jargon)

## State Reporting Requirements {#state-reporting}

### Colorado State Grant Reporting
- Typically quarterly or semi-annual progress reports
- Financial reporting on expenditures by category
- Outcome data specific to funded objectives
- May require data entry into state systems (varies by agency)
- Check: Does the proposal demonstrate awareness of state reporting expectations?

### Community Foundation Reporting
- Usually annual reports (simpler than federal/state)
- Narrative report on program progress and outcomes
- Financial report showing how grant funds were spent
- May include site visits
- Photos and stories often welcomed
- Check: Does the proposal include a realistic evaluation plan that would support foundation reporting?

## Local Match and Cost-Share {#local-match}

### When Match is Required
- Some state grants require local match (cash or in-kind)
- Community foundations generally do not require match but value leveraged funds
- In-kind match must be documented and valued appropriately

### Match Documentation Requirements
- Cash match: must be verifiable from organization's financial records
- In-kind match: must be valued at fair market value
- Volunteer time: must be documented with rates consistent with similar work
- Check: If match is required, is it clearly identified with sources and values?

### Common Match Errors
- Counting the same funds as match for multiple grants
- Overvaluing in-kind contributions
- Including match sources not yet confirmed
- Not distinguishing cash match from in-kind match

## Colorado-Specific Regulations {#colorado-regs}

### Programs Serving Youth in Colorado
- Colorado Child Protection Act requirements
- Background check requirements for adults working with minors (CBI/FBI)
- Mandatory reporting obligations under Colorado law
- Colorado Department of Education coordination if school-based
- Check: Does the proposal describe background check and mandatory reporting procedures?

### Programs Serving Veterans in Colorado
- Coordination with Colorado Department of Military and Veterans Affairs (DMVA)
- Awareness of Rocky Mountain Regional VA Medical Center resources
- Connection to Colorado's veteran service organization network
- Check: Does the proposal demonstrate knowledge of Colorado veteran resources?

### Colorado Data Privacy
- Colorado Privacy Act (CPA) effective July 2023
- Applies to organizations processing personal data of Colorado residents
- Requires data protection assessments for high-risk processing
- Stricter requirements for sensitive data (health, biometric, minor data)
- Check: If collecting personal data, does the proposal address Colorado Privacy Act?

### Colorado Nonprofit Requirements
- Must be registered with Colorado Secretary of State
- Must be registered for charitable solicitations if applicable
- Must comply with Colorado Revised Nonprofit Corporation Act
- Annual report filing required
- Check: Is the organization in good standing with Colorado Secretary of State?
