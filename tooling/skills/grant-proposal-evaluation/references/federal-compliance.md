## 2 CFR 200 Uniform Guidance {#2-cfr-200}

The single most important compliance framework for federal grants. Every federal grant recipient must comply.

### Key Requirements to Check in Proposals:

**Financial Management (200.302)**
- Proposal must demonstrate adequate financial management systems
- Must track expenditures by grant and cost category
- Must be able to produce accurate, current, and complete financial reports

**Internal Controls (200.303)**
- Organization must have internal controls that provide reasonable assurance funds are managed properly
- Check: Does the proposal describe financial oversight mechanisms?

**Cost Principles (200.400-475)**
- All costs must be: necessary, reasonable, allocable, and consistently treated
- Direct costs: clearly identifiable to a specific grant objective
- Indirect costs: must have negotiated indirect cost rate agreement (NICRA) or use de minimis rate (10% of MTDC)
- Check: Does the budget distinguish direct vs. indirect costs correctly?

**Procurement (200.317-327)**
- Competitive procurement required for goods/services above micro-purchase threshold ($10,000)
- Check: If the proposal includes subcontracts or major purchases, is procurement approach described?

**Subrecipient Monitoring (200.332)**
- If the proposal includes subawards, the applicant must describe how they'll monitor subrecipients
- Check: Are subaward relationships vs. contractor relationships properly categorized?

**Audit Requirements (200.500-521)**
- Organizations spending $750,000+ in federal awards in a fiscal year must have a Single Audit
- Check: Has the organization had a Single Audit? Any findings?

### Common 2 CFR 200 Red Flags:
- No mention of financial management systems
- Indirect cost rate used without NICRA on file
- Budget includes unallowable costs (entertainment, alcohol, lobbying, first-class travel)
- Subawards not identified or monitoring plan absent
- No description of how funds will be tracked separately from other revenue

## SAM.gov and Pre-Award Requirements {#sam-gov}

### Registration Requirements:
- Active SAM.gov registration (must be current, not expired)
- Unique Entity ID (UEI) -- replaced DUNS number
- Check: Is the applicant's SAM registration current? UEI provided?

### Certifications and Representations:
- Debarment and suspension certification
- Drug-free workplace certification
- Lobbying certification (SF-LLL if applicable)
- Check: Are required certifications addressed or included?

### FFATA (Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act):
- Awards of $30,000+ must be reported in USAspending.gov
- Check: Is the applicant aware of FFATA reporting requirements?

## SAMHSA-Specific Requirements {#samhsa}

Relevant for suicide prevention, substance abuse, and mental health grants.

### Common SAMHSA NOFO Requirements:
- Evidence-based practices (EBPs) or promising practices must be identified
- GPRA (Government Performance and Results Act) data collection required
- Cultural competence plan required
- Must address health disparities and underserved populations
- Must describe sustainability plan beyond grant period

### SAMHSA Data Requirements:
- Participation in SAMHSA's National Outcome Measures (NOMs) system
- GPRA tool administration at intake, 6-month follow-up, and discharge
- Performance metrics tied to SAMHSA's strategic priorities
- Check: Does the evaluation plan include SAMHSA-specific measures?

### SAMHSA Suicide Prevention Specific:
- Must align with National Strategy for Suicide Prevention
- Must use recognized screening tools (Columbia Protocol, PHQ-9, etc.)
- Must have protocols for imminent risk response
- Must describe connection to 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline
- Must address lethal means safety
- Check: For veteran/youth suicide prevention programs, are all of these addressed?

### SAMHSA Technology Platform Considerations:
- If using technology for crisis response (e.g., apps, bots, platforms):
  - Must describe data security measures
  - Must address 42 CFR Part 2 (substance abuse treatment records confidentiality)
  - Should address FedRAMP authorization for cloud services storing federal data
  - Check: Is the technology platform compliant with federal data security requirements?

## VA Grant-Specific Requirements {#va-grants}

### VA Innovation and SSVF Grants:
- Must serve veterans as defined by 38 U.S.C.
- Must verify veteran status through approved means
- Must describe how services complement (not duplicate) VA services
- Must show awareness of local VA Medical Center and regional resources
- Check: Does the proposal describe veteran verification and VA coordination?

### VA Reporting Requirements:
- Must use VA-specified reporting tools and timelines
- Must report on veteran-specific outcomes
- Must track and report veteran demographic data
- Check: Does the evaluation plan align with VA reporting requirements?

## DOL/Workforce Development Requirements {#dol}

Relevant for SkillBridge-adjacent programs and workforce grants.

### Key DOL Requirements:
- Must describe employer engagement strategy
- Must report on DOL Common Measures (entered employment, retention, earnings)
- Must comply with WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act) where applicable
- Must describe how services reach underserved populations
- Check: If workforce development is a program component, are DOL measures included?

## Budget and Cost Principles {#budget}

### Required Budget Components:
- **Personnel**: Salary/wage rates must be reasonable and consistent with org pay scales
- **Fringe Benefits**: Must be based on actual rates, not estimates
- **Travel**: Must justify all travel (purpose, destination, cost basis)
- **Equipment**: Items over $5,000 per unit require specific justification
- **Supplies**: Distinguish from equipment; justify quantities
- **Contractual/Subawards**: Identify all subrecipients; describe selection process
- **Other Direct Costs**: Itemize and justify each
- **Indirect Costs**: Use NICRA rate or 10% de minimis; identify MTDC base

### SF-424 Family of Forms:
- SF-424: Application for Federal Assistance (cover page)
- SF-424A: Budget Information (non-construction)
- SF-424B: Assurances (non-construction)
- SF-LLL: Disclosure of Lobbying Activities
- Check: Are all required forms included and consistent with narrative budget?

### Common Budget Red Flags:
- Budget total doesn't match amount requested on SF-424
- Math errors in budget detail
- Costs not tied to specific program activities
- Indirect cost rate applied but no NICRA on file
- Personnel time allocations exceed 100% across all funding sources
- No budget narrative (just numbers without justification)
- Supplanting (using federal funds to replace existing funding)

## Data and Privacy Requirements {#data-privacy}

### Programs Serving Minors:
- COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) if collecting data from children under 13 online
- FERPA if partnering with educational institutions
- Parental consent requirements for data collection
- Check: If the program serves youth, are minor-specific privacy protections described?

### Programs Involving Crisis/Health Data:
- HIPAA if collecting protected health information
- 42 CFR Part 2 if substance abuse treatment is involved
- State-specific health data privacy laws
- Check: Does the proposal describe how sensitive data will be protected?

### Technology and Data Security:
- FedRAMP for cloud services storing federal data
- NIST 800-53 security controls for information systems
- Encryption requirements (at rest and in transit)
- Check: If technology is a major component, are security measures adequate?

## Reporting and Monitoring {#reporting}

### Standard Federal Reporting:
- SF-425 Financial Report (quarterly or semi-annual)
- Performance progress reports (timeline varies by agency)
- Final report at grant close-out
- Check: Does the proposal demonstrate awareness of reporting requirements?

### Monitoring Readiness:
- Federal agencies conduct site visits and desk reviews
- Must maintain documentation for all expenditures
- Must be able to demonstrate progress toward stated objectives
- Check: Does the proposal describe record-keeping and documentation practices?
