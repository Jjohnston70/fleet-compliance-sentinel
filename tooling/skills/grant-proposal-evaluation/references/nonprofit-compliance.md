This reference covers compliance requirements for nonprofit organizations applying for grants, with emphasis on 501(c)(3) operations, fiscal sponsor arrangements, and program-level governance.

## 501(c)(3) Requirements {#501c3}

### Basic Eligibility for Grant Funding
- Must have active 501(c)(3) determination letter from IRS
- Must be in good standing with state of incorporation
- Must be current on IRS Form 990 filings
- Must not be on SAM.gov exclusion list (for federal grants)
- Check: Is the applying organization's tax-exempt status current and verifiable?

### When Operating Under a Parent Organization
If a program operates under a parent 501(c)(3) (e.g., FOB under ALYP):
- The parent org's EIN and determination letter are used for the application
- The parent org is the legal recipient of grant funds
- The parent org is responsible for compliance and reporting
- The program must be described as a program OF the parent organization
- Board resolution or letter authorizing the program should be included
- Check: Is the relationship between the program and parent org clearly described?

### IRS Compliance
- Form 990 must be filed annually (990, 990-EZ, or 990-N depending on revenue)
- Unrelated business income must be reported on Form 990-T
- Significant changes in activities must be reported
- Private benefit and private inurement are prohibited
- Check: Are recent 990s available and consistent with proposal claims?

## Fiscal Sponsor Arrangements {#fiscal-sponsor}

When an organization doesn't have its own 501(c)(3) status, a fiscal sponsor can serve as the legal grant recipient.

### Types of Fiscal Sponsorship
- **Model A (Direct Project)**: Fiscal sponsor owns the project; project has no separate legal identity
- **Model C (Pre-Approved Grant Relationship)**: Fiscal sponsor makes grants to the sponsored org for approved purposes

### Key Considerations for Grant Applications
- Must clearly identify the fiscal sponsor and their EIN
- Must include fiscal sponsorship agreement or MOU
- Must describe the fiscal sponsor's role in financial management
- Must show the fiscal sponsor has agreed to this specific grant
- Funders may have policies about fiscal sponsorship (some won't fund through sponsors)
- Check: If a fiscal sponsor is involved, is the arrangement clearly documented?

### Fiscal Sponsor Red Flags
- No formal fiscal sponsorship agreement
- Fiscal sponsor doesn't have capacity to manage additional grants
- Fiscal sponsor takes an unreasonable administrative fee (typical: 5-15%)
- Fiscal sponsor's 990 shows financial instability
- Grant application doesn't mention the fiscal sponsor at all

## Board Governance {#board-governance}

### What Funders Look For
- Active, engaged board of directors (not just names on paper)
- Board diversity (skills, demographics, community representation)
- Board members with relevant expertise (finance, legal, program area)
- Regular board meetings with documented minutes
- Board-approved annual budget
- Conflict of interest policy
- Check: Does the proposal demonstrate active board engagement?

### Board Resolutions
Many funders require or prefer:
- Board resolution authorizing the grant application
- Board resolution approving the program being funded
- Board approval of budget and matching funds commitments
- Check: Are required board resolutions included?

### Governance Documents Funders May Request
- Articles of incorporation
- Bylaws
- Board member list with affiliations
- Conflict of interest policy
- Whistleblower policy
- Document retention policy
- Financial policies and procedures manual
- Check: Can the organization produce these documents if requested?

## Program-Specific Compliance {#program-compliance}

### Programs Serving Minors
- Background checks required for all staff and volunteers working with youth
- Written policies for supervision and safety of minors
- Parental/guardian consent procedures
- Mandatory reporting training for all staff
- COPPA compliance if collecting data from children under 13 online
- FERPA compliance if partnering with schools
- Age-appropriate programming policies
- Check: Does the proposal address all youth protection requirements?

### Programs Involving Crisis Intervention
- Must describe protocols for imminent danger situations
- Must have referral pathways to licensed professionals
- Must describe staff training in crisis response
- Must have clear escalation procedures
- Must describe connection to 988 or other crisis lines
- Must address liability and risk management
- Check: Does the proposal have adequate crisis response protocols?

### Programs Involving Technology Platforms
- Data security and privacy policies
- Terms of service and user agreements
- Accessibility compliance (ADA, Section 508 for federal)
- Data retention and deletion policies
- Incident response plan for data breaches
- Check: If technology is central to the program, are these policies addressed?

### Programs Involving Peer Support
- Peer support worker certification requirements (varies by state)
- Scope of practice boundaries (what peers can and cannot do)
- Supervision requirements for peer workers
- Confidentiality training and agreements
- Check: Does the proposal describe peer worker qualifications and supervision?

## Financial Health Indicators {#financial-health}

Funders evaluate organizational financial health. These metrics matter:

### Key Financial Metrics
- **Operating reserve**: Months of operating expenses available (3-6 months is healthy)
- **Revenue diversification**: Multiple funding sources reduce risk
- **Program expense ratio**: Percentage of spending on programs vs. admin/fundraising
- **Debt-to-asset ratio**: Lower is generally better for nonprofits
- **Revenue trend**: Growing, stable, or declining year-over-year

### Financial Documents Funders May Request
- Most recent audited financial statements (required by many funders for orgs over $500K revenue)
- Current year organizational budget
- Program-specific budget for the proposed grant
- Most recent Form 990
- Bank statements (less common, but some funders request)

### Financial Red Flags in Grant Applications
- Organization budget doesn't show capacity to manage the requested grant amount
- No diversified revenue (100% dependent on one source)
- Recent audit findings or qualified opinion
- Significant deficit spending without explanation
- Grant request represents majority of organization's total budget (over-reliance risk)
- No financial management staff or outsourced accounting described
- Check: Does the financial picture support the organization's ability to manage the requested funds?

### For New or Small Organizations
- Acknowledge limited financial history but describe financial management plans
- Describe any fiscal oversight (accountant, bookkeeper, fiscal sponsor oversight)
- Show board engagement in financial oversight
- Describe plans for building financial capacity
- Check: For newer orgs, is financial capacity addressed with a growth plan?
