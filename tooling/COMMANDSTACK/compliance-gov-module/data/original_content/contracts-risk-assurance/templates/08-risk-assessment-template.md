Risk Assessment Methodology

Risk Identification
Identify potential risks through: Interviews, workshops, document review, threat intelligence, historical data, industry research

Risk Analysis
Analyze each risk to determine: Likelihood of occurrence, Impact if occurs, Existing controls, Control effectiveness

Risk Evaluation
Evaluate risk level based on likelihood and impact
Risk level determines priority and treatment approach

Risk Treatment
Determine appropriate treatment for each risk: Accept, Mitigate, Transfer, Avoid

Risk Likelihood Scale

Very Low (1)
Likelihood: Less than 5% probability of occurrence within assessment period
Description: Highly unlikely to occur

Low (2)
Likelihood: 5-25% probability of occurrence within assessment period
Description: Unlikely to occur

Medium (3)
Likelihood: 25-50% probability of occurrence within assessment period
Description: May occur

High (4)
Likelihood: 50-75% probability of occurrence within assessment period
Description: Likely to occur

Very High (5)
Likelihood: Greater than 75% probability of occurrence within assessment period
Description: Highly likely to occur

Risk Impact Scale

Very Low (1)
Impact: Minimal impact on operations, reputation, or finances
Financial: Less than $1,000
Operational: Minimal disruption (less than 1 hour)
Reputational: No public awareness
Compliance: No regulatory impact

Low (2)
Impact: Minor impact on operations, reputation, or finances
Financial: $1,000 - $10,000
Operational: Minor disruption (1-8 hours)
Reputational: Limited public awareness
Compliance: Minor regulatory concern

Medium (3)
Impact: Moderate impact on operations, reputation, or finances
Financial: $10,000 - $50,000
Operational: Moderate disruption (8-24 hours)
Reputational: Moderate public awareness
Compliance: Regulatory notification required

High (4)
Impact: Significant impact on operations, reputation, or finances
Financial: $50,000 - $250,000
Operational: Significant disruption (1-3 days)
Reputational: Significant public awareness and media coverage
Compliance: Regulatory investigation possible

Very High (5)
Impact: Severe impact on operations, reputation, or finances
Financial: Greater than $250,000
Operational: Severe disruption (more than 3 days)
Reputational: Severe public awareness and extensive media coverage
Compliance: Regulatory fines or penalties likely

Common Information Security Risks

Data Breach
Description: Unauthorized access to or disclosure of sensitive data
Threat Source: External attackers, malicious insiders, accidental disclosure
Vulnerability: Weak access controls, unencrypted data, phishing susceptibility
Existing Controls: Access controls, encryption, MFA, security awareness training, DLP
Typical Likelihood: Medium (3)
Typical Impact: High (4)
Typical Risk Level: High (12)

Ransomware Attack
Description: Malware encrypts data and demands ransom for decryption
Threat Source: External attackers
Vulnerability: Unpatched systems, phishing susceptibility, inadequate backups
Existing Controls: Antivirus, patch management, email filtering, backups, security awareness training
Typical Likelihood: Medium (3)
Typical Impact: High (4)
Typical Risk Level: High (12)

Insider Threat
Description: Malicious or negligent actions by employees or contractors
Threat Source: Internal personnel
Vulnerability: Excessive access privileges, inadequate monitoring, lack of awareness
Existing Controls: Least privilege access, access reviews, monitoring, background checks, security awareness training
Typical Likelihood: Low (2)
Typical Impact: High (4)
Typical Risk Level: Medium (8)

System Outage
Description: Unplanned system downtime affecting business operations
Threat Source: Technical failure, natural disaster, cyber attack
Vulnerability: Single points of failure, inadequate redundancy, lack of disaster recovery
Existing Controls: Redundancy, monitoring, disaster recovery plan, backups
Typical Likelihood: Medium (3)
Typical Impact: Medium (3)
Typical Risk Level: Medium (9)

Compliance Violation
Description: Failure to comply with regulatory requirements
Threat Source: Inadequate controls, lack of awareness, process failures
Vulnerability: Complex regulations, changing requirements, inadequate training
Existing Controls: Compliance management framework, policies and procedures, training, audits
Typical Likelihood: Low (2)
Typical Impact: High (4)
Typical Risk Level: Medium (8)

Phishing Attack
Description: Social engineering attack to steal credentials or install malware
Threat Source: External attackers
Vulnerability: User susceptibility, lack of awareness, inadequate email filtering
Existing Controls: Email filtering, security awareness training, MFA, phishing simulations
Typical Likelihood: High (4)
Typical Impact: Medium (3)
Typical Risk Level: High (12)

Vendor Security Incident
Description: Security incident at third-party vendor affecting [COMPANY_NAME] or clients
Threat Source: Vendor security weaknesses
Vulnerability: Inadequate vendor security, lack of vendor oversight
Existing Controls: Vendor security assessments, contractual security requirements, vendor monitoring
Typical Likelihood: Low (2)
Typical Impact: High (4)
Typical Risk Level: Medium (8)

Risk Treatment Options

Accept
Description: Accept the risk without additional controls
When to use: Risk level is very low or low, and cost of mitigation exceeds benefit
Documentation: Document risk acceptance decision and rationale
Approval: Risk acceptance requires CISO approval

Mitigate
Description: Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact of risk
When to use: Risk level is medium, high, or very high
Examples: Implement technical controls, enhance processes, provide training
Documentation: Document mitigation plan, controls implemented, residual risk

Transfer
Description: Transfer risk to third party (e.g., insurance, outsourcing)
When to use: Risk level is high or very high, and transfer is cost-effective
Examples: Cyber liability insurance, cloud service provider, managed security service
Documentation: Document transfer mechanism, residual risk

Avoid
Description: Eliminate the risk by not engaging in the activity
When to use: Risk level is very high and cannot be adequately mitigated or transferred
Examples: Not pursuing high-risk project, not using high-risk technology
Documentation: Document avoidance decision and rationale
