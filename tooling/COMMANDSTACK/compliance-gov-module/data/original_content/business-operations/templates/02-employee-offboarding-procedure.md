EMPLOYEE OFFBOARDING PROCEDURE

Purpose

This Employee Offboarding Procedure establishes [COMPANY_LEGAL_NAME]'s process for offboarding departing employees, contractors, and consultants. This procedure ensures departing personnel exit smoothly, company assets are recovered, access is revoked, and knowledge is transferred to protect company interests and maintain security.

Scope

This procedure applies to all departing [COMPANY_NAME] employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary personnel regardless of reason for departure (resignation, termination, retirement, end of contract). It applies to all personnel involved in the offboarding process.

Governance and Cross-Package References

This procedure integrates security and data protection requirements into the employee offboarding process.

Offboarding Process

Notification and Planning

Step 1 — Receive Departure Notification
Departure notification received from employee or manager
Notification includes: Departure date, reason for departure, notice period
Notification triggers offboarding process
Notification documented

Step 2 — Create Offboarding Plan
Offboarding plan created
Plan includes: Timeline, tasks, responsibilities, knowledge transfer plan, access revocation schedule
Plan tailored to departure circumstances (voluntary vs involuntary)
Plan documented

Step 3 — Notify Relevant Personnel
Relevant personnel notified of departure: CISO, IT, HR, team members, clients (if appropriate)
Notification timing based on departure circumstances
Confidentiality maintained as appropriate
Notifications documented

Knowledge Transfer and Transition

Step 4 — Identify Knowledge Transfer Needs
Knowledge transfer needs identified
Needs include: Project status, client information, processes, passwords, documentation
Knowledge transfer critical for business continuity
Needs documented

Step 5 — Conduct Knowledge Transfer Sessions
Knowledge transfer sessions conducted between departing employee and designated personnel
Sessions cover: Project handoff, process documentation, client introductions, system access
Sessions documented
Knowledge captured in written form

Step 6 — Document Processes and Procedures
Departing employee documents processes and procedures
Documentation includes: Step-by-step instructions, tips and tricks, contacts, resources
Documentation reviewed for completeness
Documentation stored in knowledge base

Step 7 — Transfer Responsibilities
Responsibilities transferred to other personnel
Responsibilities include: Projects, clients, tasks, system administration
Transfers communicated to relevant parties
Transfers documented

Step 8 — Introduce Replacement or Transition Contact
Replacement or transition contact introduced to clients and stakeholders (if applicable)
Introduction ensures continuity of relationships
Introduction conducted professionally
Introduction documented

Access Revocation and Security

Step 9 — Determine Access Revocation Timing
Access revocation timing determined based on departure circumstances
Voluntary departure: Access revoked on last day or after knowledge transfer
Involuntary termination: Access revoked immediately
Timing balances security and business needs
Timing documented

Step 10 — Revoke System Access
System access revoked: Google Workspace account disabled, GCP access removed, application access removed, VPN access disabled
Access revocation follows least privilege principle (most critical access first)
Access revocation documented
Access revocation verified

Step 11 — Disable User Accounts
User accounts disabled in all systems
Account disabling prevents login
Accounts retained for data retention period before deletion
Account status documented

Step 12 — Change Shared Passwords and Credentials
Shared passwords and credentials changed if known by departing employee
Credentials include: Shared accounts, API keys, service account keys
Credential changes prevent unauthorized access
Credential changes documented

Step 13 — Revoke Physical Access
Physical access revoked (if applicable): Office keys, access cards, building access
Physical access revocation prevents unauthorized entry
Physical access revocation documented

Step 14 — Review Data Access and Exfiltration Risk
Data access reviewed for potential exfiltration risk
Review includes: Recent file downloads, email forwards, external sharing
Suspicious activity investigated
Review documented

Asset Recovery

Step 15 — Recover Company Equipment
Company equipment recovered: Laptop, monitor, keyboard, mouse, mobile devices, accessories
Equipment recovery scheduled for last day or before
Equipment condition assessed
Equipment recovery documented

Step 16 — Recover Company Documents and Data
Company documents and data recovered
Recovery includes: Physical documents, USB drives, external hard drives, personal device data
Data deletion from personal devices verified
Recovery documented

Step 17 — Recover Security Credentials
Security credentials recovered: Hardware security keys, access cards, badges, tokens
Credential recovery prevents unauthorized access
Credential recovery documented

Step 18 — Recover Company Property
Other company property recovered: Business cards, company credit cards, office supplies, keys
Property recovery ensures company assets protected
Property recovery documented

Step 19 — Backup or Transfer Employee Data
Employee data backed up or transferred before account deletion
Data includes: Emails, files, project data
Data retention per Records Management Policy
Data backup documented

Administrative and HR Tasks

Step 20 — Conduct Exit Interview
Exit interview conducted by hiring manager or HR
Interview covers: Reason for departure, feedback on company, suggestions for improvement, return of property confirmation
Interview provides valuable feedback
Interview documented

Step 21 — Process Final Payroll
Final payroll processed
Final paycheck includes: Salary through last day, unused vacation payout (if applicable), expense reimbursements
Final paycheck timing per legal requirements
Payroll processing documented

Step 22 — Terminate Benefits
Benefits terminated per plan requirements
Benefits termination communicated to employee
COBRA information provided (if applicable)
Benefits termination documented

Step 23 — Collect Signed Documents
Signed documents collected: Exit interview form, property return acknowledgment, confidentiality reminder, non-compete reminder (if applicable)
Documents confirm offboarding completion
Documents stored in personnel file

Step 24 — Update Personnel Records
Personnel records updated: Employment status, termination date, termination reason, offboarding completion
Records maintained per retention policy
Records secured

Communication and Client Management

Step 25 — Communicate Departure Internally
Departure communicated to internal team
Communication timing based on circumstances
Communication professional and respectful
Communication documented

Step 26 — Communicate Departure to Clients
Departure communicated to clients (if employee had client contact)
Communication includes: Departure date, transition contact, continuity assurance
Communication maintains client confidence
Communication documented

Step 27 — Update Contact Lists and Documentation
Contact lists and documentation updated to remove departing employee
Updates include: Email distribution lists, organizational charts, project documentation, website
Updates ensure accurate information
Updates documented

Post-Departure Tasks

Step 28 — Monitor for Unauthorized Access Attempts
Monitoring for unauthorized access attempts by former employee
Monitoring includes: Login attempts, VPN connections, system access
Suspicious activity investigated and addressed
Monitoring documented

Step 29 — Delete Accounts After Retention Period
User accounts deleted after retention period (typically 30-90 days)
Account deletion permanent
Account deletion per Records Management Policy
Account deletion documented

Step 30 — Conduct Offboarding Review
Offboarding process reviewed for completeness
Review includes: All tasks completed, all assets recovered, all access revoked
Issues identified and addressed
Review documented

Step 31 — Archive Offboarding Documentation
Offboarding documentation archived
Documentation includes: Offboarding checklist, exit interview, asset recovery records, access revocation records
Documentation retained per retention policy
Documentation secured
