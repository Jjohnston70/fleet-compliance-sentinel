EMPLOYEE ONBOARDING PROCEDURE

Purpose

This Employee Onboarding Procedure establishes [COMPANY_LEGAL_NAME]'s process for onboarding new employees, contractors, and consultants. This procedure ensures new personnel are properly integrated, trained, and equipped to perform their roles effectively and securely.

Scope

This procedure applies to all new [COMPANY_NAME] employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary personnel. It applies to all personnel involved in the onboarding process.

Governance and Cross-Package References

This procedure integrates security and compliance requirements into the employee onboarding process, ensuring all personnel understand their obligations.

Compliance Requirements Integration:
All new personnel must complete security awareness training per internal-compliance skill (Security Awareness Training Program)
All new personnel must acknowledge policies including Acceptable Use Policy, Data Handling Standard, and Privacy Management Policy
Onboarding form must collect: Classification level access required, regulatory obligations (HIPAA, FedRAMP, etc.), client-specific requirements
Background checks required per internal-compliance skill (Access Control Policy) based on access level and client requirements

Cross-Package Integration:
security-governance skill: Master governance document establishing security and compliance requirements
internal-compliance skill (Security Awareness Training Program): Required training for all new personnel
internal-compliance skill (Acceptable Use Policy): Policy acknowledgment required during onboarding
data-handling-privacy skill: Data handling training required for personnel with data access
data-handling-privacy skill (Privacy Management Policy): Privacy training required for personnel handling personal information

Data Retention and Deletion:
Employee records retention follows data-handling-privacy skill (Records Management Policy)
Employee data deletion upon offboarding follows data-handling-privacy skill

For security training requirements, see internal-compliance skill (Security Awareness Training Program).
For data handling requirements, see data-handling-privacy skill.

Pre-Onboarding (Before Start Date)

Step 1 — Complete Hiring Process
Employment offer accepted
Employment agreement signed
Start date confirmed
Hiring manager notified

Step 2 — Initiate Background Check
Background check initiated (if required)
Background check includes: Criminal history, employment verification, education verification, credit check (if applicable)
Background check completed before start date
Background check results documented

Step 3 — Collect Pre-Employment Documents
Documents collected: Signed employment agreement, W-4 tax form, I-9 employment eligibility verification, direct deposit authorization, emergency contact information, NDA (if not in employment agreement)
Documents reviewed for completeness
Documents stored securely

Step 4 — Order Equipment and Supplies
Equipment ordered: Laptop, monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset, etc.
Equipment configured and tested
Supplies ordered: Business cards, office supplies, etc.
Equipment and supplies ready for start date

Step 5 — Provision Accounts and Access
User accounts created: Google Workspace, GCP (if needed), project management tools, communication tools
Access provisioned based on job role and least privilege
Temporary password generated
Account provisioning documented

Step 6 — Prepare Workspace
Physical workspace prepared (if applicable): Desk, chair, office space
Remote workspace support arranged (if remote)
Workspace ready for start date

Step 7 — Schedule Onboarding Activities
Onboarding schedule created
Schedule includes: Orientation, training sessions, meetings with team, check-ins
Schedule shared with new employee and relevant personnel
Calendar invites sent

Step 8 — Send Welcome Email
Welcome email sent to new employee
Email includes: Start date, start time, location or remote login instructions, first day schedule, contact information, what to bring
Email sets positive tone

Day 1 — First Day

Step 9 — Welcome and Orientation
New employee welcomed by hiring manager or mentor
Office tour provided (if applicable)
Introductions to team members
Company overview and culture discussed

Step 10 — Complete Administrative Tasks
Employment documents finalized
I-9 verification completed
Benefits enrollment initiated (if applicable)
Payroll setup verified

Step 11 — Distribute Equipment and Credentials
Equipment distributed: Laptop, accessories
Credentials provided: Email, temporary password, MFA setup instructions
Equipment setup assistance provided
Equipment receipt documented

Step 12 — Account Setup and MFA Enrollment
User logs into Google Workspace account
Password changed from temporary to permanent (per Password Policy)
Multi-factor authentication (MFA) enrolled (required)
Account setup verified

Step 13 — Conduct Security and Compliance Training
Security and compliance training conducted by CISO
Training covers: Information Security Policy, Acceptable Use Policy, Data Handling Policy, Password Policy, Incident Reporting
Training materials provided
Training completion documented

Step 14 — Review Policies and Procedures
Key policies and procedures reviewed: Employee handbook (if applicable), security policies, data handling procedures, communication guidelines
Policy acknowledgment forms signed
Acknowledgments stored in personnel file

Step 15 — Provide Role-Specific Training
Role-specific training provided by hiring manager
Training covers: Job responsibilities, tools and systems, processes and procedures, project overview
Training materials provided
Training documented

Step 16 — Assign Initial Tasks
Initial tasks assigned to new employee
Tasks appropriate for first day or week
Tasks provide hands-on learning
Tasks monitored by hiring manager or mentor

Week 1 — First Week

Step 17 — Continue Training and Onboarding
Additional training sessions conducted
Training covers: Advanced topics, specific tools, client information (if applicable)
Training schedule followed
Training progress monitored

Step 18 — Schedule One-on-One Meetings
One-on-one meetings scheduled with: Hiring manager, team members, CISO, other key personnel
Meetings provide opportunity to ask questions and build relationships
Meeting notes documented

Step 19 — Assign Mentor or Buddy
Mentor or buddy assigned to new employee
Mentor provides guidance and support
Mentor checks in regularly
Mentor relationship documented

Step 20 — Provide Access to Resources
Access to resources provided: Documentation, knowledge base, project files, communication channels
Resources organized and accessible
Resource locations communicated

Step 21 — Conduct First Week Check-In
Check-in meeting conducted by hiring manager
Check-in covers: Progress, questions, concerns, feedback
Issues addressed promptly
Check-in documented

Month 1 — First Month

Step 22 — Continue Role Integration
New employee integrated into role and team
Responsibilities gradually increased
Independence encouraged with support available
Integration progress monitored

Step 23 — Conduct 30-Day Review
30-day review meeting conducted by hiring manager
Review covers: Performance, progress, goals, feedback, questions, concerns
Performance expectations clarified
Review documented

Step 24 — Gather Onboarding Feedback
Feedback gathered from new employee on onboarding process
Feedback includes: What went well, what could be improved, suggestions
Feedback used to improve onboarding process
Feedback documented

Step 25 — Complete Onboarding Checklist
Onboarding checklist reviewed and completed
All tasks verified as complete
Checklist signed by new employee and hiring manager
Checklist stored in personnel file

Ongoing — Continued Development

Step 26 — Conduct Regular Check-Ins
Regular check-ins conducted by hiring manager (weekly or bi-weekly initially, then monthly)
Check-ins cover: Progress, goals, challenges, support needs
Check-ins support continued development

Step 27 — Provide Ongoing Training
Ongoing training provided as needed
Training covers: New skills, new tools, new processes, compliance updates
Training supports professional development
Training documented

Step 28 — Conduct Performance Reviews
Performance reviews conducted per Performance Management Procedure
Reviews provide feedback and set goals
Reviews support career development
Reviews documented
