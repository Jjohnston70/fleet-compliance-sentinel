MANDATORY REPORTING PROCEDURE

Purpose

This Mandatory Reporting Procedure establishes [COMPANY_NAME]'s requirements and procedures for reporting incidents, violations, and other events to federal agencies as required by law, regulation, or contract terms. This procedure ensures timely and accurate reporting to maintain compliance and support federal mission requirements.

Scope

This procedure applies to all [COMPANY_NAME] personnel, contractors, and subcontractors performing work on federal contracts. This procedure covers identification of reportable events, notification timelines, reporting channels, documentation requirements, and follow-up procedures.

Reportable Events

Security Incidents

Data Breach or Suspected Data Breach
Definition: Unauthorized access, use, disclosure, or loss of federal data
Reporting Timeline: Within 1 hour of discovery
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Contracting Officer's Representative, Agency Security Office
Required Information: Nature of breach, data affected, number of records, containment actions

Cyber Incident
Definition: Malicious cyber activity affecting federal systems or data
Reporting Timeline: Within 1 hour of discovery
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Security Office, US-CERT
Required Information: Incident type, affected systems, impact assessment, response actions

Unauthorized Access Attempt
Definition: Detected attempt to gain unauthorized access to federal systems or data
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of discovery
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Security Office
Required Information: Source of attempt, systems targeted, detection method, mitigation actions

Loss or Theft of Equipment Containing Federal Data
Definition: Physical loss or theft of devices or media containing federal information
Reporting Timeline: Within 2 hours of discovery
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Security Office, Local Law Enforcement
Required Information: Description of equipment, data contained, encryption status, circumstances of loss

Malware or Ransomware Incident
Definition: Detection of malicious software affecting systems handling federal data
Reporting Timeline: Within 1 hour of discovery
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Security Office, US-CERT
Required Information: Malware type, affected systems, containment status, recovery actions

Compliance Violations

Privacy Violation
Definition: Violation of privacy requirements affecting Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of discovery
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Privacy Office
Required Information: Nature of violation, individuals affected, corrective actions

HIPAA Violation
Definition: Violation of HIPAA Privacy or Security Rules
Reporting Timeline: Within 60 days (or per contract requirements if shorter)
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Privacy Office, HHS Office for Civil Rights
Required Information: Nature of violation, individuals affected, breach risk assessment

Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) Spillage
Definition: Unauthorized disclosure or mishandling of CUI
Reporting Timeline: Within 2 hours of discovery
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Security Office
Required Information: CUI category, extent of spillage, recipients, containment actions

Export Control Violation
Definition: Violation of export control laws or regulations
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of discovery
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Legal Office, Appropriate Regulatory Agency
Required Information: Nature of violation, items or information involved, parties involved

Classified Information Spillage
Definition: Unauthorized disclosure of classified information to unclassified systems or personnel
Reporting Timeline: Immediate (within 1 hour)
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Security Office, Facility Security Officer
Required Information: Classification level, extent of spillage, personnel exposed, containment actions

Fraud, Waste, and Abuse

Suspected Fraud
Definition: Suspected fraudulent activity related to federal contracts
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of discovery
Reporting Channel: Agency Inspector General, Contracting Officer
Required Information: Description of suspected fraud, parties involved, supporting evidence

Waste of Government Resources
Definition: Wasteful use of federal funds or resources
Reporting Timeline: Within 5 business days of discovery
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Inspector General
Required Information: Description of waste, estimated impact, recommendations for correction

Abuse of Authority or Resources
Definition: Misuse of position or resources for personal gain
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of discovery
Reporting Channel: Agency Inspector General, Contracting Officer
Required Information: Description of abuse, parties involved, supporting evidence

Conflicts of Interest

Organizational Conflict of Interest
Definition: Situation where contractor has conflicting roles or relationships
Reporting Timeline: Immediately upon identification
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer
Required Information: Nature of conflict, parties involved, proposed mitigation

Personal Conflict of Interest
Definition: Personal financial or other interest that conflicts with contract performance
Reporting Timeline: Immediately upon identification
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Ethics Office
Required Information: Nature of conflict, individual involved, proposed resolution

Contract Performance Issues

Failure to Meet Contract Requirements
Definition: Inability to meet contract deliverables, milestones, or performance standards
Reporting Timeline: As soon as known, minimum 5 business days before deadline
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Contracting Officer's Representative
Required Information: Affected deliverables, reason for delay, proposed recovery plan

Cost Overruns or Schedule Delays
Definition: Significant deviation from approved budget or schedule
Reporting Timeline: Within 5 business days of identification
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer
Required Information: Variance amount, cause of variance, corrective action plan

Subcontractor Performance Issues
Definition: Subcontractor failure to meet requirements or compliance issues
Reporting Timeline: Within 5 business days of identification
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer
Required Information: Subcontractor name, nature of issue, corrective actions

Personnel Issues

Loss of Key Personnel
Definition: Departure or extended unavailability of key personnel identified in contract
Reporting Timeline: Within 5 business days of knowledge
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer
Required Information: Personnel name and role, reason for departure, replacement plan

Security Clearance Issues
Definition: Suspension, revocation, or denial of required security clearance
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of notification
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Facility Security Officer
Required Information: Individual affected, clearance level, reason for action, impact on contract

Criminal Charges Against Personnel
Definition: Criminal charges filed against personnel working on federal contract
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of knowledge
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer
Required Information: Individual name, nature of charges, impact on contract performance

Legal and Regulatory Matters

Litigation Involving Federal Contract
Definition: Lawsuit or legal action related to federal contract performance
Reporting Timeline: Within 5 business days of service of legal documents
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Legal Office
Required Information: Nature of litigation, parties involved, potential impact

Regulatory Investigation or Inquiry
Definition: Investigation by regulatory agency related to contract performance
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of notification
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer
Required Information: Investigating agency, subject of investigation, requested information

Subpoena or Court Order
Definition: Receipt of subpoena or court order related to federal contract or data
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of receipt
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Agency Legal Office
Required Information: Issuing authority, information requested, response deadline

Environmental, Health, and Safety Incidents

Workplace Injury or Illness
Definition: Serious workplace injury or occupational illness
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of occurrence
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer (if contract-specific requirement)
Required Information: Nature of injury, circumstances, corrective actions

Environmental Incident
Definition: Spill, release, or environmental violation
Reporting Timeline: Immediate for serious incidents, within 24 hours for others
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, Appropriate Environmental Agency
Required Information: Nature of incident, environmental impact, response actions

Safety Violation
Definition: Violation of safety requirements or standards
Reporting Timeline: Within 24 hours of identification
Reporting Channel: Contracting Officer, OSHA (if applicable)
Required Information: Nature of violation, corrective actions, prevention measures

Reporting Procedures

Step 1 — Event Identification and Assessment
Personnel identify potential reportable event
Immediate supervisor notified
Event assessed against reportable event criteria
Severity and impact determined
Reporting timeline identified

Step 2 — Initial Notification
Appropriate reporting channels identified based on event type
Initial notification made within required timeline
Notification includes all required information available at time of report
Verbal notification followed by written notification
Confirmation of receipt obtained

Step 3 — Detailed Reporting
Comprehensive written report prepared
All required information included
Supporting documentation attached
Report submitted through appropriate channels
Copy maintained in company records

Step 4 — Follow-Up Reporting
Status updates provided as required by agency or contract
Additional information provided as it becomes available
Final report submitted upon event resolution
Lessons learned documented
Corrective actions implemented and reported

Step 5 — Documentation and Record Keeping
All reports and communications documented
Records maintained per federal retention requirements
Reports available for audit or inspection
Confidentiality maintained as required
Records protected from unauthorized access

Reporting Channels

Primary Reporting Channels

Contracting Officer (CO)
Use For: Contract performance issues, general notifications, most reportable events
Contact Method: Email, phone, contract-specified portal
Response Time: Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours

Contracting Officer's Representative (COR)
Use For: Technical issues, day-to-day contract matters, performance concerns
Contact Method: Email, phone, regular status meetings
Response Time: Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours

Agency Security Office
Use For: Security incidents, data breaches, cyber incidents
Contact Method: 24/7 security operations center, email, phone
Response Time: Immediate acknowledgment for critical incidents

Agency Privacy Office
Use For: Privacy violations, PII breaches, HIPAA incidents
Contact Method: Email, phone, incident reporting portal
Response Time: Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours

Agency Inspector General (IG)
Use For: Fraud, waste, abuse, criminal activity
Contact Method: IG hotline, email, secure reporting portal
Response Time: Varies by agency

US-CERT (United States Computer Emergency Readiness Team)
Use For: Cyber incidents affecting federal systems
Contact Method: us-cert.gov reporting portal, phone
Response Time: Immediate acknowledgment

Specialized Reporting Channels

HHS Office for Civil Rights
Use For: HIPAA violations
Contact Method: Online portal, mail
Response Time: Per HIPAA breach notification requirements

Department of Commerce Bureau of Industry and Security
Use For: Export control violations
Contact Method: Online reporting, phone
Response Time: Per regulatory requirements

OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration)
Use For: Serious workplace injuries, safety violations
Contact Method: Phone, online reporting
Response Time: Per OSHA requirements

Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)
Use For: Environmental incidents
Contact Method: National Response Center, regional offices
Response Time: Immediate for serious incidents
