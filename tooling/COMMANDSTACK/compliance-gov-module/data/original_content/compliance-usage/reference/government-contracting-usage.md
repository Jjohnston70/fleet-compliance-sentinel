# GOVERNMENT CONTRACTING USAGE GUIDE

## Purpose

This usage guide explains how, when, and why each document in the Government Contracting skill is used. This guide helps personnel understand the practical application of federal contracting documents.

## Skill Overview

The Government Contracting skill contains 9 comprehensive documents that establish federal overlay on top of general security, privacy, and cloud skills. These documents are essential for:
- Federal contract proposals and capability demonstrations
- Government security and compliance requirements
- Federal data handling and FOIA compliance
- NIST and FedRAMP-adjacent controls
- Mandatory reporting to federal agencies
- Federal contract closeout and offboarding
- Government-specific roles and procurement

This skill serves as the "federal layer" that modifies general policies where federal requirements override.

## Document Usage Guide

### 01 CAPABILITY STATEMENT

**How Used**
- Presents company overview and federal contracting qualifications
- Documents core competencies and technical capabilities
- Lists NAICS codes and certifications
- Provides company contact and registration information
- Demonstrates federal contracting readiness

**When Needed**
- During federal RFP and RFQ responses
- When introducing company to federal agencies
- During capability briefings and matchmaking events
- When registering on agency vendor lists
- During teaming and subcontracting discussions
- When updating SAM.gov profile
- During small business outreach events

**Why Needed**
- Required for federal contracting opportunities
- Demonstrates SDVOSB status and small business qualifications
- Provides quick reference for federal buyers
- Supports capability-based source selection
- Required for many federal procurement processes
- Establishes credibility with federal agencies
- Enables efficient proposal development

### 02 GOVERNMENT READINESS STATEMENT

**How Used**
- Demonstrates comprehensive security and compliance posture for federal agencies
- Documents NIST CSF alignment and FedRAMP-adjacent controls
- Lists regulatory compliance (HIPAA, SOC 2, GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Provides security control implementation status
- Establishes federal contracting readiness

**When Needed**
- During federal security assessments
- When responding to federal RFPs requiring security documentation
- During pre-award security reviews
- When pursuing contracts requiring NIST 800-53 or FedRAMP controls
- During federal capability demonstrations
- When completing federal security questionnaires
- During teaming with prime contractors

**Why Needed**
- Required for federal contracts with security requirements
- Demonstrates NIST CSF and 800-53 alignment
- Shows FedRAMP-adjacent control implementation
- Supports federal security clearance and authorization processes
- Required for DoD, VA, DHS, and other agency contracts
- Establishes security credibility with federal buyers
- Enables federal contract award

## Cross-Skill Integration

### Skill 2 (Security & Compliance Handbook):
- Skill 2 provides master governance; skill 4 provides federal overlay
- Skill 4 documents reference skill 2 as governance source
- Skill 2 applies to all engagements; skill 4 adds federal-specific requirements

### Skill 1 (Internal Compliance):
- Skill 1 provides operational security controls; skill 4 applies them to federal context
- Incident Response Plan (skill 1) integrates with Mandatory Reporting (skill 4)
- BC/DR procedures (skill 1) are basis for Contingency Plan Gov Edition (skill 4)

### Skill 3 (Data Handling & Privacy):
- Skill 3 provides general data handling; skill 4 provides federal data handling overlay
- Federal Data Handling & FOIA (skill 4) supersedes skill 3 for CUI and federal data
- Records Management (skill 3) harmonizes with federal retention requirements (skill 4)
- Breach Notification (skill 3) integrates with Mandatory Reporting (skill 4)

### Skill 5 (Cloud Platform Security):
- Skill 5 implements technical controls; skill 4 applies federal requirements
- Cloud Security Standards (skill 5) support NIST-Lite Alignment (skill 4)
- Data residency and sovereignty (skill 5) support federal data requirements (skill 4)

### Skill 6 (Business Operations):
- Skill 6 provides general business processes; skill 4 provides federal overlay
- Closeout & Offboarding Gov Edition (skill 4) is federal version of skill 6 offboarding
- Federal roles matrix (skill 4) is federal version of general roles (skill 6)

## Practical Usage Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Federal RFP Response
**Federal RFP requires capability statement and security documentation**
- Response: Use Capability Statement, Government Readiness Statement, NIST-Lite Alignment
- Supplement with: skill 2 Handbook, skill 5 cloud security standards
- Result: Comprehensive federal proposal

### Scenario 2: Federal Contract Award
**Federal contract awarded, need to establish federal-specific procedures**
- Response: Use Federal Data Handling & FOIA, Contingency Plan Gov Edition, Roles Matrix, Mandatory Reporting
- Integrate with: skill 1 operational procedures, skill 3 data handling
- Result: Federal contract ready for performance

### Scenario 3: CUI Handling Requirement
**Federal contract requires CUI handling**
- Response: Use Federal Data Handling & FOIA Policy, NIST-Lite Alignment
- Integrate with: skill 3 Data Handling Standard, skill 5 cloud encryption
- Result: Compliant CUI handling

### Scenario 4: Federal Security Incident
**Security incident occurs on federal contract**
- Response: Use Mandatory Reporting Procedure, Incident Response Plan (skill 1), Breach Notification (skill 3)
- Report to: CO, COR, US-CERT, agency-specific contacts
- Result: Compliant federal incident response

### Scenario 5: Federal Contract Closeout
**Federal contract completing, need closeout procedures**
- Response: Use Closeout & Offboarding Procedure Gov Edition
- Integrate with: Federal Data Handling & FOIA for data return/destruction, Records Management (skill 3) for retention
- Result: Compliant federal contract closeout

## Maintenance and Updates

### Annual Review:
- All skill 4 documents reviewed annually
- Review includes FAR/DFARS changes, agency policy updates, lessons learned
- Review coordinated with skill 2 Handbook review

### Triggered Updates:
- Updated when SDVOSB certification completes
- Updated when SAM.gov registration renewed
- Updated when federal regulations change (FAR, DFARS, agency-specific)
- Updated when federal contract performance reveals gaps
- Updated when NIST standards updated (CSF 2.0, 800-53 Rev 5)

### Consistency Maintenance:
- Capability Statement and Government Readiness Statement kept consistent (NAICS, UEI, CAGE, certifications)
- Federal data handling kept consistent with skill 3 baseline
- Federal roles kept consistent with skill 2 governance structure

## Conclusion

Skill 4 is the federal overlay that enables competition for and performance of federal contracts. These documents build on the strong foundation of skills 1, 2, 3, and 5, adding federal-specific requirements, processes, and language. Personnel working on federal contracts must be familiar with these documents and apply them in addition to general policies.
