ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY

Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy establishes [COMPANY_LEGAL_NAME]'s requirements for the acceptable and appropriate use of company information systems, networks, devices, data, and other technology resources. This policy protects company assets, ensures compliance with legal and regulatory requirements, and maintains a secure and productive work environment.

Scope

This policy applies to all [COMPANY_NAME] personnel, contractors, subcontractors, and any other individuals granted access to company information systems and resources. It covers all company-owned and personal devices used to access company resources, all company networks and systems, and all company data.

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

AI/ML Acceptable Use Principles
AI and machine learning tools provide significant business value but require careful governance to protect client data, maintain privacy, ensure security, and comply with regulations. This section establishes baseline requirements for all AI/ML usage.

Approved AI/ML Platforms
Approved AI/ML platforms per organizational standards
Cloud-based AI features - approved with data protection controls
Other AI/ML platforms require CISO approval before use
External large language models (LLMs) require explicit approval and data protection assessment

Data Protection in AI/ML Usage - Universal Principle
No Sensitive Data in AI Prompts or Inputs:
- Do NOT input PII, PHI, PCI, CUI, or other regulated identifiers into AI systems unless explicitly approved
- Do NOT paste client data, customer information, or confidential data into external AI tools
- Do NOT include passwords, API keys, tokens, or secrets in AI prompts or training data
- Do NOT upload sensitive documents or screenshots containing regulated data to AI systems

Data Anonymization Required:
- All data used for AI/ML training, testing, or inference must be anonymized or pseudonymized per data-handling-privacy skill (Anonymization Standard)
- Client data may only be used in AI systems after anonymization unless explicitly authorized in writing by client
- Use synthetic data, test data, or anonymized data for AI experimentation and development
- See data-handling-privacy skill (Anonymization Standard) for techniques to protect data in AI workflows

Acceptable AI/ML Use Cases
Business process automation and optimization
Data analysis and insights generation (using anonymized data)
Document summarization and content generation (non-sensitive content only)
Code development assistance (no secrets or sensitive data in code)
Research and learning
Approved client deliverables with proper data protection

Unacceptable AI/ML Use
Inputting client PII, PHI, PCI, or CUI into unapproved external AI tools
Using AI to process regulated data without proper anonymization
Sharing confidential business information with external AI services
Using AI to generate content that violates intellectual property rights
Using AI for illegal, unethical, or harmful purposes
Bypassing security controls or data protection requirements using AI
Relying solely on AI-generated output without human review for critical decisions

AI/ML Security Requirements
Use only approved AI/ML platforms and services
Authenticate to AI services with strong credentials and MFA
Do not share AI service accounts or credentials
Review AI-generated content for accuracy, bias, and appropriateness before use
Do not blindly trust AI-generated code, configurations, or security recommendations
Report AI security incidents or concerns immediately
Comply with client-specific AI usage restrictions

AI/ML Privacy and Compliance
All AI/ML usage must comply with GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, and applicable privacy laws
Data subject rights apply to data processed by AI systems
AI processing of personal data requires lawful basis and transparency
High-risk AI processing requires Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)
AI systems must not discriminate or create unfair bias
AI-generated decisions affecting individuals require human oversight

AI/ML Monitoring and Governance
AI/ML usage monitored for security and compliance
AI prompts and outputs subject to no-PII-in-logs principle
AI system access logged and audited
AI models and outputs reviewed for bias, fairness, and accuracy
AI governance overseen by CISO and AI Governance Committee (see cloud-platform-security skill AI Governance)
