Applicable Compliance Requirements

Information Security and Privacy Regulations

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
Applies to processing of EU personal data
Requirements: Lawful basis, data subject rights, breach notification, data protection by design, privacy impact assessments
Compliance responsibility: CISO and Data Protection Officer (if designated)
Compliance status: Implemented and monitored

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)
Applies to California resident personal information
Requirements: Privacy notice, data subject rights, opt-out, data security
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented and monitored

Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
Applies if handling Protected Health Information (PHI)
Requirements: Privacy Rule, Security Rule, Breach Notification Rule, Business Associate Agreements
Compliance responsibility: CISO and Privacy Officer (if designated)
Compliance status: Implemented when handling PHI

Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)
Applies if handling financial institution customer information
Requirements: Safeguards Rule, Privacy Rule
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented when handling financial data

Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)
Applies if handling student education records
Requirements: Privacy protections, parental rights, disclosure limitations
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented when handling education records

Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS)
Applies if processing, storing, or transmitting cardholder data
Requirements: 12 requirements covering network security, access control, monitoring, policies
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented if handling cardholder data

Government Contracting and Federal Requirements

Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR)
Applies to federal government contracts
Requirements: Contractor responsibilities, ethics, security, reporting
Compliance responsibility: CISO and Contracts Manager
Compliance status: Implemented for government contracts

Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement (DFARS)
Applies to Department of Defense contracts
Requirements: Cybersecurity, controlled unclassified information (CUI), incident reporting
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented for DoD contracts

NIST SP 800-171 (Protecting Controlled Unclassified Information)
Applies when handling CUI for federal government
Requirements: 14 families of security controls (110 controls)
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented when handling CUI

NIST Cybersecurity Framework
Voluntary framework for managing cybersecurity risk
Functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented as baseline security framework

Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)
Applies to federal agency records and contractors handling federal records
Requirements: Records management, disclosure procedures
Compliance responsibility: CISO and Records Manager
Compliance status: Implemented for government contracts

Federal Tax Information (FTI) Security Requirements (IRS Publication 1075)
Applies if handling FTI for IRS or state tax agencies
Requirements: Stringent security and privacy controls
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented if handling FTI

Industry Standards and Frameworks

ISO/IEC 27001 (Information Security Management System)
International standard for information security management
Requirements: ISMS, risk assessment, security controls, continual improvement
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Aligned with ISO 27001 (certification optional)

SOC 2 (Service Organization Control 2)
Framework for service providers managing customer data
Trust Service Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Aligned with SOC 2 (audit optional)

NIST SP 800-53 (Security and Privacy Controls)
Comprehensive catalog of security and privacy controls
Used for federal systems and high-security environments
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented for high-security requirements

CIS Controls (Center for Internet Security)
Prioritized set of cybersecurity best practices
20 controls covering basic to advanced security
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented as security baseline

Contractual and Client Requirements

Client Security Requirements
Security requirements specified in client contracts
Requirements vary by client and project
Compliance responsibility: CISO and Project Managers
Compliance status: Assessed and implemented per contract

Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs)
Confidentiality obligations in NDAs and contracts
Requirements: Protect confidential information, limit disclosure, return or destroy data
Compliance responsibility: All personnel
Compliance status: Implemented and enforced

Service Level Agreements (SLAs)
Performance and availability commitments in SLAs
Requirements: Uptime, response time, support
Compliance responsibility: Operations and Project Managers
Compliance status: Monitored and reported

Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
Data protection obligations when processing personal data for clients
Requirements: Security measures, subprocessor management, data subject rights, breach notification
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented per DPA

Business Associate Agreements (BAAs)
HIPAA obligations when handling PHI for covered entities
Requirements: Safeguards, breach notification, subcontractor management
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Implemented per BAA

State and Local Requirements

State Data Breach Notification Laws
Requirements to notify individuals and authorities of data breaches
Requirements vary by state
Compliance responsibility: CISO and Legal
Compliance status: Implemented per Breach Notification Procedure

State Privacy Laws
Various state privacy laws (Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, etc.)
Requirements similar to CCPA
Compliance responsibility: CISO
Compliance status: Monitored and implemented as applicable

State and Local Business Regulations
Business licensing, tax, employment, and other regulations
Compliance responsibility: Management and Legal
Compliance status: Implemented and maintained

Compliance Management Process

Compliance Identification and Assessment

Step 1 — Identify Applicable Requirements
Identify laws, regulations, standards, and contractual obligations applicable to organization
Consider: Industry, services, data types, clients, geographic locations
Requirements documented in compliance register

Step 2 — Assess Current Compliance
Assess current compliance with identified requirements
Gap analysis identifies areas of non-compliance or partial compliance
Assessment documented

Step 3 — Prioritize Compliance Efforts
Prioritize compliance efforts based on risk, legal obligations, and business impact
High-priority: Legal requirements, high-risk areas, client requirements
Medium-priority: Industry standards, best practices
Low-priority: Optional certifications, aspirational goals

Step 4 — Develop Compliance Plan
Develop plan to achieve and maintain compliance
Plan includes: Actions, responsibilities, timelines, resources
Plan approved by CISO and management
Plan documented and tracked

Compliance Implementation

Step 5 — Implement Controls and Processes
Implement security controls, policies, procedures, and processes to meet compliance requirements
Controls aligned with requirements
Implementation documented

Step 6 — Document Compliance
Document compliance activities and evidence
Documentation includes: Policies, procedures, records, logs, reports, assessments
Documentation organized and accessible
Documentation supports audits and assessments

Step 7 — Train Personnel
Train personnel on compliance requirements and responsibilities
Training covers: Policies, procedures, regulations, best practices
Training documented

Step 8 — Communicate Compliance
Communicate compliance requirements and expectations to personnel
Communication ongoing and reinforced
Compliance culture promoted

Compliance Monitoring and Maintenance

Step 9 — Monitor Compliance
Monitor compliance continuously through technical controls, audits, and reviews
Compliance metrics tracked
Non-compliance identified and addressed
Monitoring documented

Step 10 — Conduct Internal Audits
Conduct internal compliance audits regularly
Audits assess compliance with requirements
Audit findings documented and remediated
Audit frequency based on risk (annually or more frequently)

Step 11 — Manage Compliance Issues
Compliance issues and non-conformities identified and tracked
Issues prioritized and assigned for remediation
Issues resolved promptly
Issue resolution documented

Step 12 — Report Compliance Status
Report compliance status to management regularly
Reports include: Compliance metrics, issues, risks, improvements
Reports support decision-making and governance

Step 13 — Maintain and Improve Compliance
Compliance program maintained and continuously improved
Compliance requirements updated as laws and regulations change
Compliance processes improved based on lessons learned
Compliance program reviewed annually
