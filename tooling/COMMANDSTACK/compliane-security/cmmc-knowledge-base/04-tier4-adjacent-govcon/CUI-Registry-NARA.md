# CUI Registry and NARA Program Reference

## Overview

The CUI (Controlled Unclassified Information) Registry is the official source for CUI categories, subcategories, marking and safeguarding requirements. Maintained by the National Archives and Records Administration (NARA), the registry provides federal agencies and contractors with authoritative guidance on CUI identification, handling, and protection.

<!-- chunk: section_1 -->

## Executive Order 13556 Background

### Authority and Purpose

**Executive Order 13556** (November 4, 2010): "Controlled Unclassified Information"

**Purpose**:
- Establish uniform government-wide practices for marking and safeguarding CUI
- Reduce proliferation of classification markings and safeguarding requirements
- Create standardized CUI program replacing numerous agency-specific programs
- Provide clear guidance for contractors and federal employees

### Key Principles

1. **Standardization**: Single government-wide CUI program
2. **Transparency**: Clear, publicly available categories
3. **Efficiency**: Reduce administrative burden while maintaining security
4. **Contractor Inclusion**: Extend requirements to contractors and federal partners
5. **Interoperability**: Facilitate information sharing across agencies

### NARA as Administrator

**National Archives and Records Administration**:
- Designated as CUI executive agent
- Maintains the official CUI Registry
- Approves and authorizes CUI categories
- Provides training and guidance
- Issues CUI program policy updates

## Relationship to CMMC

CMMC Level 1-3 implementations require proper identification, handling, and protection of CUI. Understanding CUI categories and safeguarding requirements is essential for contractors seeking CMMC certification.

## CUI Categories and Subcategories

### Category Structure

The CUI Registry organizes CUI into **30+ authorized categories** and **numerous subcategories**, each with:
- Official category name and abbreviation
- Definition and description
- Applicable agencies
- Marked/Unmarked designations
- Safeguarding requirements
- Dissemination controls
- Decontrolling procedures

### CUI Categories (Primary List)

#### Agriculture Related Information (AGR)

**Coverage**:
- Information concerning agriculture, food, and farming
- Food security and supply information
- Agricultural research data

**Safeguarding Focus**:
- Protection of agricultural security information
- Food supply chain protection
- Trade-sensitive agricultural data

#### Biological Safety and Security (BIO)

**Coverage**:
- Information on biological research and safety
- Pathogen research and samples
- Biosafety and biosecurity procedures
- Pandemic preparedness information

**Safeguarding Focus**:
- Prevention of dual-use research misuse
- Protection of pathogen information
- Biosafety level (BSL) procedures

#### Chemical/Radiation Safety and Security (CRS)

**Coverage**:
- Chemical safety information
- Radiation source and safety data
- Chemical facility security plans
- Industrial chemical processes

**Safeguarding Focus**:
- Chemical facility security information
- Hazardous materials handling
- Radiation source tracking

#### Critical Infrastructure (CII)

**Coverage**:

**Safeguarding Focus**:

#### Defense Information (DEF)

**Coverage**:

**Safeguarding Focus**:
- Weapons systems security

#### Export Control Information (ECI)

**Coverage**:

**Safeguarding Focus**:

#### Intelligence-Related Information (INT)

### Marking Designations

#### CUI-Basic

**Definition**: CUI that is generally not required to have additional controls beyond standard safeguarding.

**Characteristics**:
- May be marked or unmarked
- Agencies may designate as "CUI Basic"
- Standard safeguarding sufficient (NIST 800-171 Level 1)
- FAR 52.204-21 controls adequate for protection

**Examples**:
- Unclassified federal employee directory information
- Non-sensitive federal guidance documents
- General procurement information

#### CUI-Specified

**Definition**: CUI that requires additional safeguarding controls beyond standard.

**Characteristics**:
- Must be marked as "CUI//[Specified Category]"
- Requires enhanced safeguarding controls
- NIST 800-171 minimum required
- May require additional agency-specific controls
- Dissemination controls typically apply

**Examples**:
- Technical data with export control restrictions
- Export control information (ITAR, EAR)
- Law enforcement sensitive information
- Intelligence-related information
- Nuclear security information

## Marking Requirements

### CUI Marking Standards

#### Basic Marking Format

**Standard Header Format**:
```
CUI

[Category List]
```

**For Unmarked CUI**:
- Information not marked but designated as CUI by agency
- Recipient knows information is CUI based on content/context
- Safeguarding requirements still apply

**For Marked CUI**:
```
CONTROLLED UNCLASSIFIED INFORMATION

[Category]: [Subcategory if applicable]
Dissemination Control: [MARK if applicable]
```

#### Example Markings

**Defense Information**:
```
CUI
DEF
```

**Export-Controlled Technical Data**:
```
CUI
ECI//ITAR
```

**Law Enforcement Sensitive**:
```
CUI
LEI
```

**Privacy-Protected**:
```
CUI
PTI
```

### Marking Procedures

#### Document Marking

1. **Initial Marking**: Original classification/designation authority marks
2. **Header Block**: Marking on first page or cover sheet
3. **Footer/Headers**: Optional on all pages
4. **Transmittal Documents**: Include CUI marking if transmitting CUI
5. **Electronic Files**: Metadata or file properties
6. **Email**: Include CUI in subject line or body

#### Portability Markings

For CUI being transmitted outside agency:
- Marking must be clear and visible
- Categories must be specifically identified
- Dissemination controls must be stated
- Supporting documentation should explain marking rationale

#### Handling and Protection Markings

**Dissemination Control Markings**:
```
NOFORN (Not Releasable to Foreign Nationals)
NOCON (Not Releasable to Contractors)
NOPUB (Not for Public Release)
NONREQS (Not for Release to Non-U.S. Government Agencies)
```

### Marking Exceptions

#### Practicality Exceptions

Some CUI may not be individually marked if:
- Marking is impractical (databases, multimedia)
- Information is CUI based on organizational context
- Agency establishes alternative notification procedures
- Database/system is known to contain CUI

**Alternative Procedures**:
- System-level marking
- Database or collection marking
- Notification procedures instead of individual marking
- Access control as primary protection mechanism

## Safeguarding Requirements

### General Safeguarding Standards

#### CUI-Basic Safeguarding

**Minimum Requirements**:
- Standard federal security controls
- NIST 800-171 basic controls (FAR 52.204-21)
- CMMC Level 1 controls
- Access control to authorized personnel
- Encryption in transit and at rest
- Audit logging and monitoring
- Incident response procedures

**Focus Areas**:
- Identification and access control
- Data protection in transit/at rest
- System monitoring and logging
- Personnel security and training

#### CUI-Specified Safeguarding

**Enhanced Requirements**:
- NIST 800-171 moderate to advanced controls
- CMMC Level 2-3 controls depending on category
- Additional safeguarding controls per agency
- More stringent access controls
- Enhanced monitoring and auditing
- Potentially more frequent assessments

**Category-Specific Controls**:
- **Export Control (ECI)**: Foreign national access restrictions, export compliance tracking
- **Defense (DEF)**: Facility security, cleared personnel requirements
- **Intelligence (INT)**: Need-to-know restrictions, intelligence community controls
- **Law Enforcement (LEI)**: Investigation confidentiality, witness protection

### Agency-Specific Requirements

Agencies may establish safeguarding requirements more stringent than NIST 800-171:

**DoD Additional Requirements**:
- May require CMMC Level 2 or 3
- Incident reporting (DFARS 252.204-7012)
- Media preservation requirements
- Subcontractor flowdown
- Facility security requirements

**Other Agency Requirements**:
- Agency-specific security policies
- Advanced technical controls
- Personnel vetting requirements
- Training and certification requirements

### Contractor Safeguarding Obligations

**FAR 52.204-21** requires contractors to:
- Identify and categorize CUI in their systems
- Apply appropriate safeguarding controls
- Protect against unauthorized disclosure
- Maintain confidentiality and integrity
- Implement access controls
- Monitor and detect unauthorized access
- Report security incidents

**Additional DFARS Requirements**:
- DFARS 252.204-7012 for DoD contractors
- DFARS 252.204-7021 for CMMC
- Incident reporting within 72 hours
- Media preservation (90 days)
- Cyber incident investigation support

## Dissemination Controls

### Dissemination Control Markings

Dissemination controls restrict who may receive CUI:

#### NOFORN - Not Releasable to Foreign Nationals

**Application**:
- Information not authorized for release to foreign nationals
- Restrictions on sharing with international partners
- Special handling for foreign-controlled contractors

**Examples**:
- Defense technical data
- Intelligence information
- Export-controlled information
- Foreign policy sensitive information

#### NOCON - Not Releasable to Contractors

**Application**:
- Information not authorized for contractor access
- Federal government personnel only
- Contractor access restricted per policy

**Examples**:
- Some acquisition sensitive information
- Some law enforcement information
- Personnel security investigation data

#### NOPUB - Not for Public Release

**Application**:
- Information not authorized for public disclosure
- May be released to limited government/contractor audience
- Restrictions on publication or external release

**Examples**:
- Some agency-sensitive information
- Some technical/policy information
- Some procurement information

#### NONREQS - Not for Release to Non-U.S. Government Agencies

**Application**:
- Information restricted to U.S. government entities
- Contractors not included unless government contractor relationship established
- State/local government may or may not be included per marking

### Dissemination Control Implementation

Contractors must:
1. **Identify** all dissemination controls on CUI
2. **Document** who has authorization to receive marked CUI
3. **Restrict** distribution to authorized recipients only
4. **Maintain Records** of CUI dissemination
5. **Train** personnel on dissemination restrictions
6. **Verify** authorization before sharing
7. **Report Violations** of dissemination controls

### Authorized Dissemination Verification

**Before Sharing CUI**:
- Verify recipient's authorization for CUI category
- Check dissemination control restrictions
- Verify recipient's need-to-know
- Document dissemination and authorization
- Use secure transmission methods

**Contractor Responsibility**:
- Contractors responsible for ensuring authorized dissemination
- Accountability for unauthorized disclosures
- Potential contract remedies for violations
- Incident reporting for unauthorized dissemination

## Decontrolling CUI

### Decontrolling Procedures

#### When CUI May Be Decontrolled

CUI may be decontrolled when:

1. **Declination Period Expires**: CUI automatically loses control status on specified date
2. **Information Becomes Public**: Information disclosed in public domain
3. **Authority Determination**: Original authority determines no longer CUI
4. **Administrative Action**: Agency formally removes CUI designation
5. **Loss of Basis**: Information no longer meets CUI criteria

#### Decontrolling Authority

**Who May Decontrol**:
- Original classification/designation authority
- Successor authority per agency policy
- Agency leadership per established procedures
- Through systematic decontrolling procedures

**Process**:
- Determine whether CUI basis still exists
- Verify no remaining security concerns
- Document decontrolling decision
- Notify affected parties
- Remove CUI markings from records

### Contractor Role in Decontrolling

**Contractor Limitations**:
- Contractors generally may not decontrol CUI
- Contractor identifies CUI but agency designates
- Contractor may recommend decontrolling to agency
- Contractor continues safeguarding until agency decontrols

**Exception Procedures**:
- Contractors may implement agency decontrolling procedures
- Contractors follow agency guidance for systematic decontrolling
- Contractors maintain records of decontrolling actions
- Contractors report decontrolling actions per contract requirements

## Agency CUI Programs

### Agency Program Responsibilities

Each federal agency must establish a CUI program addressing:

1. **Training and Awareness**: Personnel trained on CUI procedures
2. **Identification and Marking**: Clear procedures for marking CUI
3. **Safeguarding**: Implementation of security controls
4. **Dissemination Control**: Procedures for controlled distribution
5. **Incident Response**: Procedures for unauthorized disclosure
6. **Oversight and Compliance**: Program monitoring and compliance verification
7. **Records and Documentation**: Maintenance of CUI records

### Senior Agency Official for Records Management (SAORM)

Each agency designates a SAORM responsible for:
- CUI program development and implementation
- Policy and procedure establishment
- Personnel training and awareness
- Compliance monitoring and oversight
- Incident investigation and reporting
- Coordination with NARA

### Contractor Coordination with Agencies

Contractors must:
1. **Understand** agency CUI program requirements
2. **Comply** with agency-specific safeguarding procedures
3. **Report** through agency-designated incident reporting channels
4. **Coordinate** with agency on CUI management
5. **Document** compliance with agency program
6. **Support** agency audits and compliance reviews

## CUI and CMMC Alignment

### CUI in CMMC Assessments

**Level 1 Assessment**:
- Assumes contractor has FCI or CUI
- Does not specifically assess CUI marking/safeguarding
- Focuses on 15 basic safeguarding controls
- Verifies technical controls (encryption, access control, etc.)

**Level 2 Assessment**:
- Evaluates identification of CUI in systems
- Assesses marking procedures and implementation
- Verifies safeguarding of identified CUI
- Reviews incident handling of CUI incidents
- Checks compliance with agency requirements

**Level 3 Assessment**:
- Enhanced focus on CUI protection
- Assessment of advanced threat identification
- CUI-specific incident response procedures
- Supply chain protection of CUI

### CUI Identification in CMMC

Contractors undergoing CMMC assessment must:
1. **Identify**: All CUI in contractor systems
2. **Categorize**: Specific CUI categories present
3. **Mark**: Appropriately mark CUI per standards
4. **Safeguard**: Apply appropriate controls per category
5. **Document**: Maintain records of CUI identification and handling
6. **Train**: Ensure personnel understand CUI procedures
7. **Report**: Communicate CUI handling to assessors

### Safeguarding Control Alignment

**FAR 52.204-21 and CUI-Basic**:
- The 15 FAR requirements adequate for CUI-Basic
- NIST 800-171 controls adequate for CUI-Basic
- CMMC Level 1 controls adequate for CUI-Basic

**NIST 800-171 and CUI-Specified**:
- NIST 800-171 moderate/advanced controls for CUI-Specified
- CMMC Level 2 controls adequate for most CUI-Specified
- CMMC Level 3 for most sensitive CUI-Specified categories

## Common Implementation Issues

### Issue 1: Unclear CUI Designation

**Problem**: Contractors unclear what information is CUI vs. not CUI

**Solutions**:
- Review agency CUI program guidance
- Ask agency point of contact for classification
- Document agency designation decisions
- Conduct CUI identification workshop
- Use CUI Registry to understand categories

### Issue 2: Marking Inconsistency

**Problem**: CUI not consistently marked throughout organization

**Solutions**:
- Develop clear marking procedures
- Implement automated marking where possible
- Train all personnel on marking procedures
- Conduct periodic compliance reviews
- Provide marking templates
- Track marking compliance metrics

### Issue 3: Dissemination Control Violations

**Problem**: CUI with dissemination controls shared with unauthorized recipients

**Solutions**:
- Implement access controls restricting CUI access
- Establish authorization verification procedures
- Train personnel on dissemination controls
- Establish incident reporting procedures
- Conduct regular training updates
- Track CUI dissemination/sharing

### Issue 4: System Inventory Gaps

**Problem**: Not all systems processing CUI identified

**Solutions**:
- Conduct comprehensive system inventory
- Map data flows to identify CUI systems
- Implement system discovery tools
- Establish procedures for new systems
- Regular inventory updates and validation
- Cross-reference with security assessments
