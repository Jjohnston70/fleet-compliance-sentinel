**PART 3 --- SDVOSB APPLICATION PREP PACKET**

This packet includes:

1.  Required documents

2.  Required information

3.  Control & ownership requirements

4.  Pre-check questions

5.  Common reasons SBA denies applications

6.  How to prepare your Operating Agreement

7.  What to gather right now

This is built for SBA's current certification portal:

<https://veterans.certify.sba.gov>

**1. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS (YOU MUST HAVE ALL OF THESE)**

**Identity & Veteran Status**

-   DD-214 (Member 4 copy strongly preferred)

-   VA Disability Rating Letter (showing service-connected disability %)

**Business Formation Documents**

-   Articles of Organization (from Colorado SOS)

-   Operating Agreement (your LLC's internal rules)

-   EIN Assignment Letter (IRS CP 575 or PDF confirmation)

-   Certificate of Good Standing (optional but smart)

-   Copy of business license (if your city issues one)

**Proof of Ownership & Control**

-   Operating Agreement showing 51%+ ownership by you

-   Any membership certificates (if your LLC uses them)

-   Any amendments to organizational documents

-   Proof you serve as Owner / CEO / Managing Member (your title)

**Financial Documents**

-   Bank letter or voided check showing the business account

-   Business tax return (if filed yet; optional for new LLCs)

-   Personal tax return (SBA sometimes requests, but rare year one)

**Management & Control Documentation**

-   Resume for you (already written)

-   Statement defining your decision-making authority

-   Any contracts showing you sign on behalf of the LLC

-   Any leases (only if physical office required)

**2. REQUIRED INFORMATION (YOU WILL ENTER IN THE SBA PORTAL)**

Have these answers ready:

-   Your SSN

-   Your EIN

-   Your UEI (once SAM is active)

-   CAGE code (auto-generated)

-   Business start date

-   Percentage of ownership (must be \>51%)

-   Description of services (your DSBS text works here)

-   Number of employees (can be 1)

-   Revenue (can be \$0 for new businesses)

**3. OWNERSHIP & CONTROL REQUIREMENTS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)**

SBA rejects many SDVOSB applications because of this section.

To be approved:

-   **You must own at least 51%** of the LLC

-   **You must control all decisions**

-   **No one can overrule you**

-   **You must work full-time in the business** (SBA assumes you do
    unless proven otherwise)

-   **Your Operating Agreement must match your authority**

Control =\
You hire, fire, sign contracts, spend money, manage the business, and
decide strategy.

If anyone else (even family) has equal or greater authority, SBA will
deny.

**4. PRE-CHECK QUESTIONS (SBA WILL ASK THESE)**

Answer these before applying.

1.  Are you the highest-ranking officer? (Yes --- Owner/CEO)

2.  Do you have the most control? (Yes)

3.  Do you manage day-to-day operations? (Yes)

4.  Do you control all long-term decisions? (Yes)

5.  Are there any non-veterans with veto power? (No)

6.  Does your Operating Agreement give you unrestricted authority?
    (Should be yes)

7.  Does anyone else own part of the business? (If yes, you must own
    51%+)

If the answer to any required control question is wrong, SBA will deny
you automatically.

**5. COMMON REASONS SBA DENIES SDVOSB APPLICATIONS**

These are the top pitfalls that kill otherwise qualified applications:

1.  **Operating Agreement doesn't explicitly give the veteran full
    control**

2.  Another member can veto decisions

3.  Signature authority isn't clear

4.  Veteran doesn't appear full-time in the business

5.  Veteran has another full-time job that shows conflict

6.  Missing Articles, EIN letter, or DD-214

7.  LLC ownership percentages unclear

8.  Wrong spelling or formatting of business name across documents

9.  SAM.gov TIN mismatch

10. Business address mismatch between IRS, SAM, and SBA

We are correcting all of this now so you avoid these issues.

**6. HOW TO PREPARE YOUR OPERATING AGREEMENT (CRITICAL)**

Your Operating Agreement must include this structure:

**Ownership**

-   You: **51% or more**

-   Others (if any): minority only

**Management**

-   You must be the **sole Managing Member** or **CEO with full
    authority**.

**Decision Authority**

Operating Agreement should clearly state:

-   You have unrestricted authority to bind the company

-   You can sign all contracts

-   You control finances

-   You hire and fire

-   No vote or approval from non-veterans is required

**No Restrictions Clause**

Avoid any wording suggesting:

-   "unanimous vote required"

-   "major decisions require both members"

-   "tie-breaker by non-veteran"

-   "board of managers" if others are on it

We keep it simple.\
If you want, I can draft a compliant Operating Agreement section for
you.
