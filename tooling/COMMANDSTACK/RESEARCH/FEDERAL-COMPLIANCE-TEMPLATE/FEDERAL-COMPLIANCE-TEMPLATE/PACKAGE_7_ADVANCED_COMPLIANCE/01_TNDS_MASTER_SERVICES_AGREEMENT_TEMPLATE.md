MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT TEMPLATE

Purpose

This Master Services Agreement (MSA) Template provides True North Data Strategies LLC with a standardized legal framework for services delivered to clients. This template establishes terms and conditions that govern the relationship between True North Data Strategies and clients.

Scope

This template applies to all True North Data Strategies client engagements requiring a master services agreement. It applies to all personnel involved in contract negotiation and execution.

Governance and Cross-Package References

This MSA template integrates security, compliance, and data protection requirements into client contracts.

1. Services

1.1 Services Overview
Service Provider shall provide professional services to Client as described in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW") executed by the Parties. Each SOW shall reference this Agreement and shall specify the scope of services, deliverables, timeline, fees, and other terms specific to that engagement.

1.2 Statements of Work
Each SOW shall be in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties. Each SOW shall become part of this Agreement upon execution. In the event of conflict between this Agreement and a SOW, the SOW shall control with respect to the specific services covered by that SOW.

1.3 Service Standards
Service Provider shall perform services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards. Service Provider shall use qualified personnel with appropriate skills and experience.

1.4 Changes to Services
Changes to services described in a SOW shall be documented in a written change order signed by both Parties. Change orders shall specify the nature of the change, impact on timeline, and impact on fees.

2. Fees and Payment

2.1 Fees
Client shall pay Service Provider the fees specified in each SOW. Fees may be fixed price, time and materials, or other pricing model as specified in the SOW.

2.2 Expenses
Client shall reimburse Service Provider for reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred in performing services, provided such expenses are pre-approved by Client in writing or specified in the SOW.

2.3 Invoicing
Service Provider shall invoice Client per the schedule specified in the SOW. If no schedule is specified, Service Provider shall invoice monthly for services performed and expenses incurred during the prior month.

2.4 Payment Terms
Client shall pay invoices within [30] days of invoice date. Payments shall be made via check, ACH, wire transfer, or other method agreed by the Parties.

2.5 Late Payment
Invoices not paid within [30] days of invoice date shall accrue interest at the rate of [1.5%] per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.

2.6 Taxes
Fees do not include applicable sales, use, or other taxes. Client shall pay or reimburse Service Provider for all such taxes except taxes based on Service Provider's net income.

3. Term and Termination

3.1 Term
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until terminated by either Party as provided herein. Each SOW shall specify its own term.

3.2 Termination for Convenience
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon [30] days prior written notice to the other Party.

3.3 Termination for Cause
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or the SOW and fails to cure such breach within [15] days after receiving written notice of the breach.

3.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination, Service Provider shall cease performing services, Client shall pay for services performed and expenses incurred through the termination date, and each Party shall return or destroy confidential information of the other Party.

3.5 Survival
Sections relating to fees and payment, confidentiality, intellectual property, warranties, limitation of liability, and indemnification shall survive termination of this Agreement.

4. Confidentiality

4.1 Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party") that is marked as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

4.2 Obligations
Receiving Party shall: (a) protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care; (b) use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement; and (c) not disclose Confidential Information to third parties except as permitted herein.

4.3 Permitted Disclosures
Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those herein.

4.4 Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was rightfully known to Receiving Party without confidentiality restrictions before disclosure; (c) is rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality restrictions; or (d) is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information.

4.5 Required Disclosures
Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law or court order, provided Receiving Party gives Disclosing Party prompt notice and reasonable assistance in seeking protective order.

4.6 Return or Destruction
Upon termination or upon Disclosing Party's request, Receiving Party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information and certify such return or destruction in writing.

5. Intellectual Property

5.1 Client Materials
Client shall retain all right, title, and interest in and to materials, data, and information provided by Client to Service Provider ("Client Materials").

5.2 Deliverables
Upon Client's payment in full for services, Service Provider hereby assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in and to deliverables specifically created for Client under a SOW ("Deliverables"), including all intellectual property rights therein.

5.3 Service Provider Materials
Service Provider shall retain all right, title, and interest in and to: (a) materials, tools, methodologies, and know-how developed by Service Provider prior to or independent of this Agreement; (b) materials, tools, methodologies, and know-how developed by Service Provider in performing services that are not Deliverables; and (c) modifications and enhancements to the foregoing (collectively, "Service Provider Materials").

5.4 License to Service Provider Materials
Service Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free license to use Service Provider Materials to the extent incorporated in Deliverables.

5.5 Third-Party Materials
Service Provider shall identify any third-party materials incorporated in Deliverables. Service Provider shall obtain necessary licenses for Client's use of such third-party materials or Client shall obtain such licenses directly.

6. Warranties

6.1 Authority
Each Party represents and warrants that it has full authority to enter into this Agreement and perform its obligations hereunder.

6.2 Services Warranty
Service Provider warrants that services shall be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards. Client's exclusive remedy for breach of this warranty shall be re-performance of deficient services at no additional charge.

6.3 No Other Warranties
EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY PROVIDED HEREIN, SERVICE PROVIDER MAKES NO WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR PARTICULAR PURPOSE, OR NON-INFRINGEMENT.

7. Limitation of Liability

7.1 Limitation
EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY OR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

7.2 Cap
EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY OR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CLIENT TO SERVICE PROVIDER IN THE [12] MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

8. Indemnification

8.1 Service Provider Indemnification
Service Provider shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from and against claims, damages, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from: (a) Service Provider's breach of this Agreement; (b) Service Provider's negligence or willful misconduct; or (c) claims that Deliverables infringe third-party intellectual property rights, provided Client promptly notifies Service Provider of the claim, gives Service Provider sole control of defense and settlement, and provides reasonable assistance.

8.2 Client Indemnification
Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Service Provider from and against claims, damages, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from: (a) Client's breach of this Agreement; (b) Client's negligence or willful misconduct; or (c) claims arising from Client Materials, provided Service Provider promptly notifies Client of the claim, gives Client sole control of defense and settlement, and provides reasonable assistance.

9. Data Protection and Security

9.1 Data Processing Agreement
If Service Provider processes personal data on behalf of Client, the Parties shall execute a Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") that complies with applicable data protection laws.

9.2 Security
Service Provider shall implement and maintain reasonable administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect Client data from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

9.3 Security Incidents
Service Provider shall notify Client promptly upon becoming aware of any unauthorized access to or disclosure of Client data and shall cooperate with Client in investigating and remediating the incident.

10. General Provisions

10.1 Independent Contractors
The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing herein creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship.

10.2 Assignment
Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other Party's prior written consent, except that either Party may assign to a successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets.

10.3 Notices
Notices shall be in writing and delivered by email, courier, or certified mail to the addresses specified in this Agreement or as updated by a Party in writing.

10.4 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Colorado without regard to conflicts of law principles.

10.5 Dispute Resolution
The Parties shall attempt to resolve disputes through good faith negotiation. If negotiation fails, disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration in Colorado Springs, Colorado under the rules of the American Arbitration Association.

10.6 Entire Agreement
This Agreement, together with all SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings, whether written or oral.

10.7 Amendments
This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement signed by both Parties.

10.8 Waiver
Waiver of any provision shall not constitute waiver of any other provision or subsequent waiver of the same provision.

10.9 Severability
If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

10.10 Force Majeure
Neither Party shall be liable for failure to perform due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters, acts of war, terrorism, government actions, or internet service failures.
