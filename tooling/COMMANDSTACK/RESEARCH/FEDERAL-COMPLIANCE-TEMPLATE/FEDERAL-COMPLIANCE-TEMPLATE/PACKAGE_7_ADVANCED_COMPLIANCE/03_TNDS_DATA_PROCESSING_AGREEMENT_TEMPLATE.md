DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT TEMPLATE

Purpose

This Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Template provides True North Data Strategies LLC with a standardized legal framework for processing personal data on behalf of clients. This template ensures compliance with data protection laws including GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable regulations.

Scope

This template applies to all True North Data Strategies client engagements where True North Data Strategies processes personal data on behalf of the client. It applies to all personnel involved in data processing activities.

Governance and Cross-Package References

1. Definitions

1.1 Definitions
"Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined by applicable Data Protection Laws.

"Data Protection Laws" means all applicable laws and regulations relating to privacy and data protection, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other applicable laws.

"Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates.

"Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, transmission, erasure, or destruction.

"Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

"Security Incident" means any unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, damage, alteration, or disclosure of Personal Data.

3. Processor Obligations

3.1 Processing Instructions
Processor shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller, including with respect to transfers of Personal Data outside the jurisdiction where Controller is established, unless required by applicable law.

3.2 Compliance with Laws
Processor shall comply with all applicable Data Protection Laws in processing Personal Data.

3.3 Confidentiality
Processor shall ensure that persons authorized to process Personal Data are subject to confidentiality obligations.

3.4 Security Measures
Processor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data against Security Incidents, taking into account the state of the art, costs of implementation, nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing, and risks to Data Subjects.

3.5 Security Measures Detail
Security measures include:
Encryption of Personal Data in transit and at rest
Access controls and authentication
Network security and firewalls
Security monitoring and logging
Incident detection and response
Regular security assessments and testing
Physical security of facilities and equipment
Secure disposal of Personal Data

3.6 Sub-processors

Controller is notified of intended Sub-processor engagements
Controller has opportunity to object to Sub-processor engagement
Sub-processor is bound by data protection obligations equivalent to those in this DPA
Processor remains liable for Sub-processor's compliance

3.8 Data Subject Rights
Processor shall, to the extent legally permitted, promptly notify Controller if Processor receives a request from a Data Subject to exercise rights under Data Protection Laws. Processor shall assist Controller in responding to such requests.

3.9 Assistance to Controller
Processor shall assist Controller in ensuring compliance with obligations under Data Protection Laws, including:
Security of processing
Data protection impact assessments
Prior consultation with supervisory authorities
Notification of Security Incidents to supervisory authorities and Data Subjects

3.10 Security Incident Notification
Processor shall notify Controller without undue delay upon becoming aware of a Security Incident. Notification shall include:
Description of Security Incident
Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected
Categories and approximate number of Personal Data records affected
Likely consequences of Security Incident
Measures taken or proposed to address Security Incident and mitigate harm

3.11 Audits and Inspections
Processor shall make available to Controller information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and allow for and contribute to audits and inspections conducted by Controller or an auditor mandated by Controller, upon reasonable notice and during business hours.

3.12 Deletion or Return of Personal Data
Upon termination of services, Processor shall, at Controller's choice, delete or return all Personal Data to Controller and delete existing copies, unless applicable law requires storage of Personal Data.

4. Controller Obligations

4.1 Lawful Processing
Controller represents and warrants that it has lawful basis for processing Personal Data and for instructing Processor to process Personal Data.

4.2 Compliance with Laws
Controller shall comply with all applicable Data Protection Laws in its processing of Personal Data and in its instructions to Processor.

4.3 Instructions
Controller's instructions to Processor shall comply with applicable Data Protection Laws. Controller shall be responsible for ensuring that its instructions are lawful.

4.4 Data Subject Rights
Controller shall be responsible for responding to Data Subject requests to exercise rights under Data Protection Laws, with assistance from Processor as provided herein.

5. International Data Transfers

5.1 Data Transfer Mechanisms
If Processor processes Personal Data in a jurisdiction outside the jurisdiction where Controller is established, the Parties shall implement appropriate safeguards for such transfer as required by applicable Data Protection Laws.

5.2 Standard Contractual Clauses
If required by applicable Data Protection Laws, the Parties shall execute Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission or other applicable supervisory authority.

5.3 Additional Safeguards
Processor shall implement additional technical and organizational measures as necessary to ensure adequate protection of Personal Data transferred internationally.

6. Liability and Indemnification

6.1 Liability
Each Party shall be liable for damages caused by its breach of this DPA in accordance with the limitation of liability provisions in the Agreement.

6.2 Indemnification
Processor shall indemnify Controller for fines, penalties, and damages imposed on Controller by supervisory authorities or awarded to Data Subjects resulting from Processor's breach of this DPA.

7. Term and Termination

7.1 Term
This DPA shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for the term of the Agreement.

7.2 Termination
This DPA shall terminate upon termination of the Agreement, provided that obligations relating to deletion or return of Personal Data and confidentiality shall survive termination.

8. General Provisions

8.1 Relationship to Agreement
This DPA forms part of the Agreement. In the event of conflict between this DPA and the Agreement with respect to processing of Personal Data, this DPA shall control.

8.2 Amendments
This DPA may be amended only by written agreement signed by both Parties, provided that Processor may amend this DPA to comply with changes in Data Protection Laws upon notice to Controller.

8.3 Governing Law
This DPA shall be governed by the same law as the Agreement.

8.4 Severability
If any provision of this DPA is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.
