This template applies to all True North Data Strategies relationships requiring confidentiality protection. It applies to all personnel involved in contract negotiation and execution.

1. Purpose

1.1 Purpose of Disclosure
The Parties wish to explore a business relationship concerning: [Describe purpose, e.g., "potential consulting engagement," "partnership opportunity," "vendor relationship"]

1.2 Confidential Information Exchange
In connection with this purpose, Disclosing Party may disclose Confidential Information to Receiving Party. This Agreement governs the use and disclosure of such Confidential Information.

2. Definition of Confidential Information

2.1 Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by Disclosing Party to Receiving Party, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, that:
Is marked as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with a similar designation, or
Reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

2.2 Types of Confidential Information
Confidential Information includes, but is not limited to:
Business information: Business plans, strategies, financial information, pricing, customer lists, supplier lists, marketing plans
Technical information: Technical data, designs, specifications, software, source code, algorithms, processes, methods, know-how
Personal information: Employee information, customer information, personal data
Proprietary information: Trade secrets, inventions, discoveries, improvements, intellectual property

2.3 Exclusions from Confidential Information
Confidential Information does not include information that:
Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by Receiving Party
Was rightfully known to Receiving Party without confidentiality restrictions before disclosure by Disclosing Party
Is rightfully received by Receiving Party from a third party without confidentiality restrictions and without breach of any obligation to Disclosing Party
Is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of or reference to Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records

3. Obligations of Receiving Party

3.1 Non-Use
Receiving Party shall use Confidential Information solely for the purpose stated in Section 1.1 and for no other purpose.

3.2 Non-Disclosure
Receiving Party shall not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted by this Agreement.

3.3 Protection
Receiving Party shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

3.4 Permitted Disclosures
Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, contractors, consultants, and advisors ("Representatives") who:
Have a legitimate need to know the Confidential Information for the purpose stated in Section 1.1
Are informed of the confidential nature of the Confidential Information
Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement

3.5 Responsibility for Representatives
Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

3.6 Security Measures
Receiving Party shall implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect Confidential Information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

4. Required Disclosures

4.1 Legal Requirement
If Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, court order, or other legal process to disclose Confidential Information, Receiving Party shall:
Promptly notify Disclosing Party of such requirement before disclosure (unless prohibited by law)
Cooperate with Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy
Disclose only the minimum Confidential Information required
Use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment of disclosed Confidential Information

5. Return or Destruction of Confidential Information

5.1 Return or Destruction
Upon Disclosing Party's written request or upon termination of this Agreement, Receiving Party shall promptly:
Return to Disclosing Party all Confidential Information in tangible form (including all copies), or
Destroy all Confidential Information in tangible form (including all copies) and certify such destruction in writing

5.2 Electronic Copies
Receiving Party shall use reasonable efforts to delete or destroy all electronic copies of Confidential Information, except that Receiving Party may retain electronic copies that are automatically archived in accordance with its standard backup and disaster recovery procedures, provided such copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

5.3 Survival of Obligations
Receiving Party's confidentiality obligations shall survive return or destruction of Confidential Information.

6. No Rights Granted

6.1 No License
Nothing in this Agreement grants Receiving Party any license, right, title, or interest in or to Confidential Information, except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the purpose stated in Section 1.1.

6.2 No Obligation to Disclose
Nothing in this Agreement obligates Disclosing Party to disclose any Confidential Information.

6.3 No Obligation to Proceed
Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to proceed with any transaction or relationship.

7. No Warranty

7.1 No Warranty
CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION IS PROVIDED "AS IS" WITHOUT WARRANTY OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR PARTICULAR PURPOSE, OR NON-INFRINGEMENT.

7.2 No Representation
Disclosing Party makes no representation as to the accuracy or completeness of Confidential Information.

8. Remedies

8.1 Irreparable Harm
Receiving Party acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to Disclosing Party for which monetary damages may be an inadequate remedy.

8.2 Equitable Relief
In the event of breach or threatened breach of this Agreement, Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

8.3 No Waiver
No failure or delay by Disclosing Party in exercising any right or remedy shall constitute a waiver of such right or remedy.

9. Term and Termination

9.1 Term
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for [2] years, unless earlier terminated by either Party upon [30] days prior written notice to the other Party.

9.2 Survival
Receiving Party's obligations with respect to Confidential Information disclosed during the term of this Agreement shall survive termination of this Agreement for [5] years from the date of disclosure, except that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall survive for as long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

10. General Provisions

10.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties concerning the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings, whether written or oral.

10.2 Amendments
This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement signed by both Parties.

10.3 Assignment
Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other Party's prior written consent, except that either Party may assign this Agreement to a successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets.

10.4 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Colorado without regard to conflicts of law principles.

10.5 Jurisdiction
Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in Colorado Springs, Colorado.

10.6 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

10.7 Waiver
Waiver of any provision shall not constitute waiver of any other provision or subsequent waiver of the same provision.

10.8 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.

10.9 Electronic Signatures
This Agreement may be executed by electronic signature, which shall have the same force and effect as an original signature.

10.10 Notices
All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email, courier, or certified mail to the addresses specified in this Agreement or as updated by a Party in writing.

11. Export Control

11.1 Export Compliance
Receiving Party acknowledges that Confidential Information may be subject to export control laws and regulations. Receiving Party shall comply with all applicable export control laws and regulations in its use and disclosure of Confidential Information.

12. Data Protection

12.1 Personal Data
If Confidential Information includes personal data, Receiving Party shall process such personal data in compliance with applicable data protection laws and regulations.
