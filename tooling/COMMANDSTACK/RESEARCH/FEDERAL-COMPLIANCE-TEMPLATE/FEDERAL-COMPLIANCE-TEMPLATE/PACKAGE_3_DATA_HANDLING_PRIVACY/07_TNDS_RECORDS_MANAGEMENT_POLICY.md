TRUE NORTH DATA STRATEGIES LLC
RECORDS MANAGEMENT POLICY

Purpose

This Records Management Policy establishes True North Data Strategies LLC's framework for the systematic control of records throughout their lifecycle from creation or receipt through processing, distribution, maintenance, and disposal. This policy ensures records are properly managed to meet business needs, legal obligations, and regulatory requirements.

Scope

This policy applies to all True North Data Strategies personnel, contractors, subcontractors, and all records regardless of format (paper, electronic, audio, video) or location. It covers business records, client records, financial records, personnel records, legal records, and all other records created or received in the course of business operations.

Governance and Cross-Package References

This policy is the authoritative source for retention requirements across all TNDS operations. All packages defer to this policy for records retention and disposition.

Universal TNDS Principles:
Data-as-Regulated: TNDS treats all client and customer data as regulated by default; retention requirements ensure compliance
No Sensitive Data in Logs: Records management logs and documentation must not contain PII, PHI, PCI, CUI, or other regulated identifiers in unprotected form

Cross-Package Integration:
Package 2 (Security & Compliance Handbook): Master governance document; this policy implements records management requirements
Package 3 (Data Handling Standard): Data classification determines retention requirements
Package 1 (Backup and Recovery Procedure): Backup retention aligns with this policy

For data classification, see Package 3 (Data Handling Standard).

Definitions

Record
Recorded information regardless of form or medium created, received, or maintained as evidence of business activities, transactions, or decisions. Includes documents, emails, databases, spreadsheets, presentations, contracts, reports, and other information assets.

Records Management
The systematic control of records throughout their lifecycle including creation, use, maintenance, and disposition.

Retention Period
The length of time a record must be retained to meet business, legal, or regulatory requirements.

Disposition
The action taken at the end of a record's retention period, typically destruction or permanent preservation.

Legal Hold
A suspension of normal disposition procedures for records relevant to actual or anticipated litigation, investigation, or audit.

Vital Records
Records essential to the continued operation of the organization during and after an emergency or disaster.

Official Record
The authoritative copy of a record designated for retention. Duplicates may be destroyed when no longer needed for business purposes.
