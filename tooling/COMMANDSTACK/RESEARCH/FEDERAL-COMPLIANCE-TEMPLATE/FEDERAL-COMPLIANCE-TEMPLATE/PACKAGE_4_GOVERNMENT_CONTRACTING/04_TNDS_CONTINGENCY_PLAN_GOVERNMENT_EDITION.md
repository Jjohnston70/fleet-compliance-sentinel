TRUE NORTH DATA STRATEGIES LLC
CONTINGENCY PLAN (GOVERNMENT EDITION)

Purpose

This Contingency Plan establishes True North Data Strategies LLC's procedures for maintaining continuity of operations and recovering from disruptions when supporting federal government contracts. This plan ensures mission-critical functions continue during emergencies, disasters, or other disruptions.

Scope

This plan applies to all True North Data Strategies operations supporting federal government contracts. It covers business continuity, disaster recovery, alternate facilities, personnel succession, and testing procedures to ensure uninterrupted support to federal agencies.

Disruption Scenarios and Response Procedures

Scenario 1 — Natural Disaster (Fire, Flood, Earthquake)

Immediate Actions (0-2 hours)
Ensure personnel safety
Assess facility damage and accessibility
Activate alternate work location (remote work)
Notify federal contracting officer of disruption
Assess impact on contract deliverables

Short-Term Actions (2-24 hours)
Restore access to mission-critical systems
Verify data integrity and availability
Establish communication with federal agency
Implement alternate facility if needed
Provide status update to contracting officer

Long-Term Actions (24+ hours)
Assess facility repair timeline
Implement long-term alternate facility if needed
Resume normal contract performance
Document disruption and recovery actions
Conduct lessons learned review

Scenario 2 — Cyber Incident or Data Breach

Immediate Actions (0-1 hour)
Isolate affected systems
Activate incident response team
Assess scope and impact of incident
Notify federal contracting officer per contract requirements
Preserve evidence for investigation

Short-Term Actions (1-8 hours)
Contain and eradicate threat
Restore systems from clean backups
Verify data integrity
Implement additional security controls
Provide detailed incident report to federal agency

Long-Term Actions (8+ hours)
Complete forensic investigation
Implement remediation measures
Update security controls and procedures
Coordinate with federal agency on lessons learned
Resume normal operations with enhanced monitoring

Scenario 3 — Key Personnel Unavailability

Immediate Actions (0-4 hours)
Activate personnel succession plan
Notify backup personnel
Assess impact on contract deliverables
Notify federal contracting officer if significant impact
Redistribute workload to available personnel

Short-Term Actions (4-24 hours)
Implement backup assignments
Engage subcontractors if needed
Adjust project timelines if necessary
Maintain communication with federal agency
Document personnel changes

Long-Term Actions (24+ hours)
Recruit replacement personnel if needed
Update succession plan based on lessons learned
Ensure knowledge transfer and documentation
Resume normal staffing levels
Conduct after-action review

Scenario 4 — Pandemic or Public Health Emergency

Immediate Actions (0-24 hours)
Activate remote work for all personnel
Ensure secure remote access capability
Assess personnel health and availability
Notify federal contracting officer of remote work status
Verify continued access to mission-critical systems

Short-Term Actions (1-7 days)
Establish remote work routines and schedules
Maintain regular communication with federal agency
Monitor personnel health and availability
Adjust project timelines if personnel unavailable
Implement additional health and safety measures

Long-Term Actions (7+ days)
Sustain remote work operations
Maintain contract performance standards
Coordinate with federal agency on long-term arrangements
Update contingency plan based on experience
Plan for eventual return to normal operations

Scenario 5 — Utility Outage (Power, Internet, Communications)

Immediate Actions (0-2 hours)
Activate alternate work location (remote work)
Verify cloud system availability
Establish alternate communication methods
Notify federal contracting officer if significant impact
Assess duration of outage

Short-Term Actions (2-8 hours)
Maintain operations from alternate location
Use mobile hotspots or alternate internet if needed
Continue mission-critical work
Provide status updates to federal agency
Monitor utility restoration progress

Long-Term Actions (8+ hours)
Implement alternate facility if extended outage
Coordinate with utility providers on restoration
Resume normal operations when utilities restored
Document outage impact and response
Update contingency plan if needed

Communication Protocols During Disruptions

Federal Agency Notification

Initial Notification
Contracting Officer notified within 2 hours of significant disruption
Contracting Officer's Representative (COR) notified within 2 hours
Initial notification includes nature of disruption, expected impact, and estimated recovery time
Notification methods: Email, phone, and contract-specified channels

Status Updates
Status updates provided every 4 hours during active disruption
Daily status updates during extended disruptions
Updates include recovery progress, revised timelines, and mitigation actions
Final notification when normal operations resume

Escalation Procedures
Escalation to senior agency officials if disruption impacts mission-critical deliverables
Coordination with agency security office for cyber incidents
Coordination with agency privacy office for data breaches
Follow contract-specific escalation procedures

Internal Communication
All personnel notified of disruption and response actions
Regular team meetings during disruption response
Clear assignment of roles and responsibilities
Documentation of all communication and decisions

External Communication
Subcontractors and partners notified as needed
Coordination with cloud service providers
Communication with facility management
Media inquiries referred to federal agency

Data Backup and Recovery Procedures

Backup Schedule
Federal data backed up hourly to geographically separate location
Contract deliverables backed up daily
Financial and administrative data backed up daily
Full system backups performed weekly
Backup integrity verified monthly

Backup Storage
Primary backups stored in cloud infrastructure (Google Cloud Platform)
Secondary backups stored in alternate cloud region
Encryption applied to all backups
Access controls limit backup access to authorized personnel
Backup retention per federal requirements and contract terms

Recovery Procedures
Recovery procedures documented and tested quarterly
Recovery from backup can be initiated within 2 hours
Full system recovery achievable within RTO targets
Data integrity verification performed after recovery
Recovery procedures updated based on test results
