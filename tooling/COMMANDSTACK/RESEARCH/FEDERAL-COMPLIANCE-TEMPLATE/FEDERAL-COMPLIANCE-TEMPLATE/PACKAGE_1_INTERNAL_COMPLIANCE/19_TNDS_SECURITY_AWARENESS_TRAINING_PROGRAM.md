TRUE NORTH DATA STRATEGIES LLC
SECURITY AWARENESS TRAINING PROGRAM

Purpose

This Security Awareness Training Program establishes True North Data Strategies LLC's requirements for educating personnel about information security risks, policies, and best practices. This program ensures all personnel understand their security responsibilities and can recognize and respond to security threats.

Scope

This program applies to all True North Data Strategies personnel including employees, contractors, subcontractors, and any other individuals with access to company systems or data. It covers all security awareness training activities from onboarding through ongoing education.

Governance and Cross-Package References

This program operates under the authority of the Security and Compliance Handbook (Package 2), which establishes the master governance framework for security awareness and training.

Universal TNDS Principles (Critical Training Topics):
Data-as-Regulated: TNDS treats all client and customer data as regulated by default - all personnel must understand data protection requirements
No Sensitive Data in Logs: Personnel must understand that logs, prompts, debugging output, comments, and screenshots must NEVER contain PII, PHI, PCI, CUI, or other regulated identifiers

Cross-Package Integration:
Package 2 (Security & Compliance Handbook): Master governance document establishing security awareness requirements
Package 1 (Acceptable Use Policy): Establishes acceptable use requirements covered in training
Package 3 (Data Handling & Privacy): Data protection requirements that must be included in training
Package 6 (Employee Onboarding Procedure): Integrates security training into onboarding process

For acceptable use requirements, see Package 1 (Acceptable Use Policy).
For data protection training content, see Package 3 (Data Handling Standard).
For onboarding integration, see Package 6 (Employee Onboarding Procedure).

Phishing Simulation Program

Program Overview
Simulated phishing emails sent to personnel
Tests ability to recognize and report phishing
Provides education and feedback
Conducted quarterly
Results tracked and reported

Simulation Process

Step 1 — Campaign Planning
Phishing simulation campaign planned
Email templates selected (based on current threats)
Target audience identified (all personnel or specific groups)
Campaign schedule determined

Step 2 — Email Delivery
Simulated phishing emails sent
Emails mimic real phishing tactics
Emails contain safe links (no actual malware)
Emails sent during business hours

Step 3 — User Response Tracking
User actions tracked (clicked link, entered credentials, reported email)
Tracking anonymous or aggregated to protect privacy
Results recorded in simulation platform

Step 4 — Immediate Feedback
Users who click link receive immediate education
Education explains phishing indicators
Education reinforces reporting process
Feedback constructive, not punitive

Step 5 — Remedial Training
Users who fail simulation receive additional training
Training covers phishing recognition
Training reinforces best practices
Training completion tracked

Step 6 — Results Analysis
Campaign results analyzed
Metrics: Click rate, reporting rate, failure rate
Trends identified
Results compared to previous campaigns

Step 7 — Reporting
Results reported to management
Report includes metrics, trends, recommendations
Individual results kept confidential
Report used to improve training program

Simulation Best Practices
Simulations realistic but not overly deceptive
Simulations educational, not punitive
Simulations varied (different tactics, senders, topics)
Simulations aligned with current threats
Simulations conducted regularly (quarterly)
