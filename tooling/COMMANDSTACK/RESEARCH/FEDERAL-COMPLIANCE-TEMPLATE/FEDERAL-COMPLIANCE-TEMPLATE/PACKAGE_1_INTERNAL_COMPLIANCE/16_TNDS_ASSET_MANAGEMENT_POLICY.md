TRUE NORTH DATA STRATEGIES LLC
ASSET MANAGEMENT POLICY

Purpose

This Asset Management Policy establishes True North Data Strategies LLC's requirements for identifying, tracking, managing, and protecting information technology and information assets throughout their lifecycle. This policy ensures assets are properly inventoried, secured, maintained, and disposed of.

Scope

This policy applies to all True North Data Strategies IT assets including hardware, software, data, and information assets. It applies to all personnel responsible for acquiring, using, managing, or disposing of company assets.

Governance and Cross-Package References

This policy operates under the authority of the Security and Compliance Handbook (Package 2), which establishes the master governance framework for asset management.

Universal TNDS Principles:
Data-as-Regulated: TNDS treats all client and customer data as regulated by default; asset management must track and protect assets containing regulated data
No Sensitive Data in Logs: Asset inventory and management records must not contain PII, PHI, PCI, CUI, or other regulated identifiers in unprotected form

Cross-Package Integration:
Package 2 (Security & Compliance Handbook): Master governance document establishing asset management requirements
Package 3 (Data Handling & Privacy): Data classification determining asset protection requirements
Package 1 (Encryption and Key Management): Encryption requirements for assets containing sensitive data
Package 3 (Records Management Policy): Retention requirements for asset management records

For data classification of assets, see Package 3 (Data Handling Standard).
For encryption of assets, see Package 1 (Encryption and Key Management).

Asset Criticality

Assets essential for business operations
Failure would cause significant business impact
Require highest level of protection and availability

High Priority Assets
Assets important for business operations
Failure would cause moderate business impact

Medium Priority Assets

Failure would cause minor business impact
Require standard protection

Low Priority Assets
Assets with minimal business impact

Require basic protection
Examples: Test systems, archived data, reference materials
