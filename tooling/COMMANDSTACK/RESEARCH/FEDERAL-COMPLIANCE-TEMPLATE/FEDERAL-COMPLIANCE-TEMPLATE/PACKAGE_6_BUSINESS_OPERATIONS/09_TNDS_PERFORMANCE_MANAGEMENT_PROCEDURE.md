TRUE NORTH DATA STRATEGIES LLC
PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT PROCEDURE

Purpose

This Performance Management Procedure establishes True North Data Strategies LLC's process for managing employee performance. This procedure ensures performance expectations are clear, performance is monitored and evaluated, feedback is provided, and performance issues are addressed to support employee success and company goals.

Scope

This procedure applies to all True North Data Strategies employees. It applies to managers, CISO, and all personnel involved in performance management.

Governance and Cross-Package References

This procedure integrates security and compliance performance expectations into the overall performance management process.

Cross-Package Integration:
Package 2 (Security & Compliance Handbook): Master governance document; security and compliance performance expectations
Package 1 (Security Awareness Training Program): Security training completion tracked in performance management

For security performance expectations, see Package 2 (Security & Compliance Handbook).

Performance Management Principles

Clear Expectations
Performance expectations clearly defined and communicated
Expectations aligned with job role and company goals
Expectations documented
Expectations understood by employees

Continuous Feedback
Feedback provided continuously, not just during formal reviews
Feedback timely, specific, and constructive
Feedback supports improvement and development
Feedback culture fostered

Fair and Objective
Performance evaluated fairly and objectively
Evaluation based on defined criteria and evidence
Bias minimized
Consistency maintained

Development Focus
Performance management supports employee development
Strengths recognized and leveraged
Development needs identified and addressed
Career growth supported

Two-Way Communication
Performance discussions are two-way conversations
Employee input sought and valued
Open and honest communication encouraged
Trust and respect maintained

Performance Management Cycle

Goal Setting

Step 1 — Define Performance Goals
Performance goals defined for each employee
Goals aligned with: Job role, company objectives, individual development
Goals SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
Goals documented

Step 2 — Communicate Performance Expectations
Performance expectations communicated clearly
Expectations include: Goals, responsibilities, standards, behaviors
Expectations discussed and agreed upon
Expectations documented

Step 3 — Develop Performance Plan
Performance plan developed
Plan includes: Goals, action steps, resources needed, timeline, success criteria
Plan supports goal achievement
Plan documented

Ongoing Performance Management

Step 4 — Monitor Performance
Performance monitored continuously
Monitoring includes: Progress toward goals, quality of work, behaviors, contributions
Monitoring informal and ongoing
Monitoring documented as needed

Step 5 — Provide Continuous Feedback
Feedback provided regularly (weekly or bi-weekly)
Feedback includes: Positive reinforcement, constructive criticism, coaching
Feedback timely and specific
Feedback documented for significant items

Step 6 — Conduct Check-In Meetings
Regular check-in meetings conducted (weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly)
Meetings discuss: Progress, challenges, support needs, feedback
Meetings maintain alignment and engagement
Meetings documented

Step 7 — Address Performance Issues Promptly
Performance issues addressed as soon as identified
Issues discussed with employee
Corrective action plan developed if needed
Issues documented

Performance Reviews

Step 8 — Conduct Mid-Year Performance Review
Mid-year review conducted (6 months after goal setting or last annual review)
Review includes: Progress toward goals, strengths, development areas, feedback
Review provides opportunity for course correction
Review documented

Step 9 — Conduct Annual Performance Review
Annual performance review conducted
Review includes: Goal achievement, overall performance, competencies, behaviors, contributions
Review comprehensive and thorough
Review documented

Step 10 — Prepare Performance Review
Manager prepares for performance review
Preparation includes: Review goals, gather evidence, assess performance, identify strengths and development areas, prepare feedback
Preparation ensures quality review

Step 11 — Conduct Performance Review Meeting
Performance review meeting conducted with employee
Meeting discusses: Performance assessment, achievements, strengths, development areas, feedback, employee input
Meeting constructive and development-focused
Meeting documented

Step 12 — Document Performance Review
Performance review documented
Documentation includes: Performance rating, achievements, strengths, development areas, goals for next period
Documentation signed by manager and employee
Documentation stored in personnel file

Performance Ratings

Rating Scale

Exceeds Expectations
Performance consistently exceeds expectations
Goals exceeded
Significant contributions made
High-quality work delivered

Meets Expectations
Performance consistently meets expectations
Goals achieved
Solid contributions made
Quality work delivered

Needs Improvement
Performance sometimes does not meet expectations
Some goals not achieved
Improvement needed in specific areas
Performance issues addressed

Unsatisfactory
Performance consistently does not meet expectations
Goals not achieved
Significant improvement needed
Performance improvement plan required

Rating Criteria
Performance rated based on: Goal achievement, quality of work, behaviors, competencies, contributions
Rating objective and evidence-based
Rating consistent across employees

Performance Improvement

Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

Step 13 — Identify Need for PIP
Need for PIP identified when performance unsatisfactory or needs improvement
PIP used to address specific performance deficiencies
PIP goal is employee success
PIP decision documented

Step 14 — Develop Performance Improvement Plan
PIP developed by manager with CISO input
PIP includes: Performance deficiencies, performance expectations, action steps, support provided, timeline (typically 30-90 days), consequences of not improving
PIP specific and actionable
PIP documented

Step 15 — Communicate PIP to Employee
PIP communicated to employee in meeting
Meeting discusses: Performance issues, expectations, plan, support, timeline, consequences
Employee input sought
Employee acknowledges PIP in writing

Step 16 — Monitor PIP Progress
PIP progress monitored closely (weekly check-ins)
Progress documented
Support and coaching provided
Adjustments made if needed

Step 17 — Evaluate PIP Outcome
PIP outcome evaluated at end of timeline
Evaluation determines: Performance improved to acceptable level, performance partially improved, performance not improved
Evaluation documented

Step 18 — Determine Next Steps
Next steps determined based on PIP outcome
Improved performance: PIP successfully completed, return to normal performance management
Partially improved: PIP extended or modified
Not improved: Employment termination considered
Next steps documented
