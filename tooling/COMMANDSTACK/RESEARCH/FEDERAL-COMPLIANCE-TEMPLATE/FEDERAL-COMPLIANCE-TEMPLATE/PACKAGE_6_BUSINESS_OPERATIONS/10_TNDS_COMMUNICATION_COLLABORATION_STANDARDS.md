TRUE NORTH DATA STRATEGIES LLC
COMMUNICATION AND COLLABORATION STANDARDS

Purpose

This Communication and Collaboration Standards document establishes True North Data Strategies LLC's standards for effective communication and collaboration. These standards ensure clear, professional, timely, and secure communication and collaboration across the organization and with clients.

Scope

These standards apply to all True North Data Strategies employees, contractors, and consultants. They apply to all internal and external communication and collaboration activities.

Governance and Cross-Package References

These standards integrate security and data protection requirements into communication and collaboration practices.

Universal TNDS Principles - Communication Application:
Data-as-Regulated: All client and customer data in communications treated as regulated by default
No Sensitive Data in Logs: Communication logs, chat transcripts, and collaboration tools must not contain PII, PHI, PCI, CUI in unprotected form

Cross-Package Integration:
Package 2 (Security & Compliance Handbook): Master governance document establishing communication security requirements
Package 3 (Data Handling Standard): Data classification and handling requirements for communications
Package 1 (Acceptable Use Policy): Acceptable use requirements for communication tools

For data handling in communications, see Package 3 (Data Handling Standard).
For acceptable use requirements, see Package 1 (Acceptable Use Policy).

Communication Channels and Standards

Email Communication

Email Standards
Professional email address used (company domain)
Subject line clear and descriptive
Message well-structured with greeting, body, closing, signature
Tone professional and courteous
Grammar and spelling checked
Attachments virus-scanned

Email Response Time
Urgent emails: Respond within 2 hours during business hours
Normal emails: Respond within 24 hours
Out-of-office: Set auto-reply when unavailable for more than 1 day

Email Security
Sensitive information encrypted or sent via secure file sharing
Phishing emails reported and deleted
Email forwarding to personal accounts prohibited
Email retention per policy

Email Best Practices
Use "Reply All" judiciously
Keep distribution lists current
Use BCC for large recipient lists to protect privacy
Avoid email for urgent matters (use phone or instant messaging)
Proofread before sending

Instant Messaging

Instant Messaging Tools
Google Chat for internal communication
Client-approved tools for client communication

Instant Messaging Standards
Status set accurately (Available, Busy, Away)
Messages professional and clear
Urgent matters escalated to phone or video call
Sensitive information not shared via instant messaging
Message history retained per policy

Instant Messaging Response Time
Available status: Respond within 15 minutes
Busy status: Respond when available
Away status: Respond when returned

Instant Messaging Best Practices
Use for quick questions and coordination
Avoid for complex discussions (use email or meeting)
Respect status indicators
Use threads to keep conversations organized

Phone Communication

Phone Standards
Answer professionally with name and company
Speak clearly and at appropriate volume
Listen actively
Take notes during calls
Return voicemails within 24 hours

Phone Best Practices
Use phone for urgent matters
Use phone for sensitive or complex discussions
Schedule calls when possible
Confirm understanding and next steps
Follow up with email summary for important calls

Video Conferencing

Video Conferencing Tools
Google Meet for internal and client meetings
Client-approved tools for client meetings

Video Conferencing Standards
Join meetings on time
Mute when not speaking
Use video when possible (professional appearance and background)
Minimize distractions
Test audio and video before important meetings

Video Conferencing Best Practices
Send agenda in advance
Start and end on time
Engage all participants
Record meetings when appropriate (with permission)
Share meeting notes and action items after meeting

Meeting Standards

Meeting Purpose

Meetings necessary and productive
Alternatives to meetings considered (email, instant messaging)

Meeting Agenda

Agenda includes: Topics, time allocations, presenters, desired outcomes
Agenda followed during meeting

Meeting Participants
Participants necessary and appropriate
Participants prepared
Participants engaged

Meeting Facilitation
Meetings start and end on time
Facilitator keeps meeting on track
All participants have opportunity to contribute
Decisions and action items documented

Meeting Documentation
Meeting notes captured
