TRUE NORTH DATA STRATEGIES LLC
DOCUMENT CONTROL PROCEDURE

Purpose

This Document Control Procedure establishes True North Data Strategies LLC's process for creating, reviewing, approving, distributing, and maintaining controlled documents. This procedure ensures documents are accurate, current, accessible, and properly managed throughout their lifecycle.

Scope

This procedure applies to all True North Data Strategies controlled documents including policies, procedures, standards, templates, plans, and other official documents. It applies to all personnel who create, review, approve, or use controlled documents.

Governance and Cross-Package References

This procedure establishes document control requirements that support the entire TNDS documentation ecosystem.

Cross-Package Integration:
Package 2 (Security & Compliance Handbook): Master governance document establishing documentation requirements
Package 3 (Records Management Policy): Document retention and disposal requirements
Package 3 (Data Handling Standard): Document classification and protection requirements

For document retention, see Package 3 (Records Management Policy).
For document classification, see Package 3 (Data Handling Standard).

Document Types and Classification

Controlled Documents

Policies
High-level statements of intent and direction
Approved by CISO or management
Reviewed annually
Examples: Information Security Policy, Privacy Policy

Procedures
Step-by-step instructions for performing activities
Approved by CISO or process owner
Reviewed annually or when process changes
Examples: Incident Response Plan, Change Management Procedure

Standards
Mandatory requirements and specifications
Approved by CISO or technical authority
Reviewed annually or when standards change
Examples: Password Policy, Encryption Standards

Templates
Standardized formats for documents or deliverables
Approved by CISO or process owner
Reviewed as needed
Examples: Project Initiation Template, SLA Template

Plans
Documents describing approach to specific activities
Approved by CISO or project sponsor
Reviewed per project or annually
Examples: Business Continuity Plan, Disaster Recovery Plan

Guidelines
Recommended practices and advice
Approved by CISO or subject matter expert
Reviewed as needed
Examples: Secure Coding Guidelines, Remote Work Guidelines

Uncontrolled Documents

Working Documents
Draft documents not yet approved
Project documents for specific projects
Personal notes and working files
Email and informal communication

Document Classification

Public Documents
Documents intended for public distribution
No confidentiality restrictions
Examples: Marketing materials, public website content

Internal Documents
Documents for internal use only
Not for external distribution without approval
Examples: Internal procedures, meeting notes

Confidential Documents
Documents containing sensitive business information
Restricted distribution
Examples: Financial information, strategic plans, client contracts

Restricted Documents
Documents containing highly sensitive information
Strictly controlled access
Examples: Security procedures, personnel records, proprietary information

Document Naming and Numbering

Document Naming Convention

Standard Format

Example: POLICY_Information_Security_v2.0
Example: PROC_Incident_Response_v1.5

Document Type Codes
POLICY: Policy documents
PROC: Procedure documents
STD: Standard documents
TMPL: Template documents

Version Numbering

Version Number Format
Major.Minor (e.g., 1.0, 1.1, 2.0)
Major version: Significant changes requiring re-approval
Minor version: Minor updates, corrections, clarifications

Version Incrementing
Initial version: 1.0
Minor updates: Increment minor version (1.0 → 1.1 → 1.2)
Major updates: Increment major version (1.9 → 2.0)
Draft versions: 0.1, 0.2, etc. (before initial approval)

Document Lifecycle

Document Creation

Step 1 — Identify Need for Document
Need for new document identified
Need based on: New process, regulatory requirement, gap analysis, improvement initiative
Need documented and approved

Step 2 — Assign Document Owner
Document owner assigned
Owner responsible for: Document creation, maintenance, accuracy
Owner documented

Step 3 — Create Draft Document
Draft document created by owner or designated author
Draft follows document structure and format standards
Draft version numbered 0.1, 0.2, etc.
Draft stored in working folder

Step 4 — Conduct Internal Review
Internal review conducted by subject matter experts and stakeholders
Reviewers provide feedback and suggestions
Feedback incorporated into draft
Review documented

Document Approval

Step 5 — Submit for Approval
Document submitted for approval when ready
Submission includes: Final draft, review comments, justification
Submission to CISO or designated approval authority

Step 6 — Conduct Approval Review
Approval authority reviews document
Review verifies: Accuracy, completeness, compliance, alignment with standards
Approval authority may request changes

Step 7 — Approve Document
Document approved by approval authority
Approval documented: Approver name, date, signature (electronic or written)
Approved document version numbered 1.0
Approval triggers document publication

Document Publication and Distribution

Step 8 — Publish Document
Approved document published to central repository
Document moved from working folder to official folder
Document accessible to authorized personnel
Publication documented

Step 9 — Distribute Document
Document distributed to relevant personnel
Distribution methods: Email notification, intranet posting, training session
Distribution ensures awareness
Distribution documented

Step 10 — Communicate Document
Document communicated to affected personnel
Communication includes: Purpose, key points, effective date, where to find
Communication ensures understanding
Communication documented

Document Maintenance

Step 11 — Monitor Document Currency
Document currency monitored
Monitoring includes: Regulatory changes, process changes, feedback, incidents
Outdated documents identified
Monitoring ongoing

Step 12 — Conduct Scheduled Review
Scheduled review conducted per review frequency (typically annually)
Review verifies: Accuracy, currency, relevance, effectiveness
Review findings documented
Review triggers update if needed

Step 13 — Update Document
Document updated when needed
Updates may be: Minor (corrections, clarifications) or major (significant changes)
Updated document follows document creation and approval process
Update version number incremented

Step 14 — Archive Superseded Version
Superseded version archived when new version published
Archived version moved to archive folder
Archived version retained per retention policy
Archival documented

Document Archival and Disposal

Step 15 — Archive Obsolete Document
Obsolete document archived when no longer needed
Archived document moved to archive folder
Archived document retained per retention policy
Archival documented

Step 16 — Dispose of Document
Document disposed of after retention period
Disposal method: Secure deletion
Disposal documented
Disposal per Records Management Policy
