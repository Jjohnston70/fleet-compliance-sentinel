TRUE NORTH DATA STRATEGIES LLC PACKAGE 4 FEDERAL AND GOVERNMENT RESEARCH DOCUMENT

Purpose

This research document identifies official requirements from authoritative U.S. federal sources for Package 4 Federal and Government documents and validates that True North Data Strategies documentation meets or exceeds these requirements.

Scope

Package 4 contains the following documents:
• Capability Statement
• Government Readiness Statement
• Federal Data Handling and FOIA Policy
• Contingency Plan Government Edition
• NIST-Lite Alignment Document
• Mandatory Reporting Procedure
• Closeout and Offboarding Procedure Government Edition
• Roles and Responsibilities Matrix Government Edition
• Procurement and Subcontractor Policy

This research focuses on federal cybersecurity, CUI handling, FOIA, reporting, contingency planning, and procurement-related requirements.

Official Sources and Requirements

Federal Information Security Modernization Act (FISMA)

Source
United States Congress and Office of Management and Budget (OMB)
Key Policy: OMB Circular A-130
Website: https://www.whitehouse.gov/omb

Relevant Requirements
• Risk-based information security for federal agencies and supporting contractors
• Use of NIST standards and guidelines
• Continuous monitoring and reporting

TNDS Compliance
• NIST-Lite Alignment Document demonstrates alignment with NIST frameworks as expected under FISMA.
• Government Readiness Statement positions TNDS to support agency-compliant security postures.

NIST Special Publications 800-53 and 800-171

Source
National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)

Key Documents
• NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations
• NIST SP 800-171 Protecting Controlled Unclassified Information in Nonfederal Systems and Organizations

Relevant Requirements
• Implementation of security controls for federal and contractor systems
• Protection of CUI in nonfederal systems
• Documentation of policies, procedures, and assessment

TNDS Compliance
• NIST-Lite Alignment Document maps TNDS controls to NIST families for moderate-impact and CUI environments.
• Federal Data Handling and FOIA Policy defines CUI handling, including marking, access, storage, transmission, and disposition consistent with 800-171.

FAR 52.204-21 Basic Safeguarding of Covered Contractor Information Systems

Source
Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR)
Website: https://www.acquisition.gov

Requirements
• Basic controls for covered contractor information systems (access control, awareness, configuration, incident response, maintenance, media protection, physical protection, system and information integrity)

TNDS Compliance
• Federal-focused policies and NIST-Lite mapping document show that required basic safeguarding controls are addressed.
• Procurement and Subcontractor Policy ensures flow-down of safeguards.

DFARS 252.204-7012 Cyber Incident Reporting and CUI

Source
Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement (DFARS)
Website: https://www.acquisition.gov/dfars

Requirements
• Adequate security using NIST SP 800-171 controls for CUI
• Mandatory reporting of cyber incidents to DoD
• Preservation and forensic support
• Subcontractor flow-down

TNDS Compliance
• Federal Data Handling and FOIA Policy and Mandatory Reporting Procedure define incident reporting and preservation obligations consistent with DFARS.
• Procurement and Subcontractor Policy supports requirement to flow down CUI protection and reporting obligations.

Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) Program

Source
National Archives and Records Administration (NARA)
Website: https://www.archives.gov/cui

Requirements
• CUI marking, handling, disseminating, decontrolling, and destruction
• Agency policies and contractual specifications for CUI

TNDS Compliance
• Federal Data Handling and FOIA Policy incorporates CUI classification, access, storage, and decontrol expectations.
• Records Management and Closeout Procedures support CUI disposition on contract completion.

Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)

Source
United States Code 5 U.S.C. § 552
Website: https://www.foia.gov

Requirements
• Public access to federal agency records subject to exemptions
• Contractor records potentially subject to FOIA via the agency

TNDS Compliance
• Federal Data Handling and FOIA Policy defines TNDS’s role in supporting agency FOIA responses while protecting confidential and proprietary information.
• Closeout and Offboarding Procedure ensures records needed for FOIA support are appropriately retained or returned.

Federal Incident and Mandatory Reporting Requirements

Sources
• Agency-specific incident reporting policies
• US-CERT / CISA reporting channels
• Sectoral regulators (e.g., HHS, VA, DoD)

TNDS Compliance
• Mandatory Reporting Procedure documents timelines, escalation paths, and reporting mechanisms for security incidents, breaches, and other reportable events.
• Contingency Plan Government Edition and Roles and Responsibilities Matrix align incident, continuity, and reporting responsibilities.
