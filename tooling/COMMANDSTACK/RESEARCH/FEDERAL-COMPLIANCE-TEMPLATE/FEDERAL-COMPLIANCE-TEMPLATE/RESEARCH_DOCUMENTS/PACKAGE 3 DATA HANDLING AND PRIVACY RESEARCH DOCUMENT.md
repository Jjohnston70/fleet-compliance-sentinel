TRUE NORTH DATA STRATEGIES LLC PACKAGE 3 DATA HANDLING AND PRIVACY RESEARCH DOCUMENT

Purpose

This research document identifies official requirements from authoritative sources for Package 3 Data Handling and Privacy documents and validates that True North Data Strategies documentation meets or exceeds these requirements.

Scope

Package 3 contains the following documents:
• Data Handling Standard
• Privacy Management Policy
• Data Lifecycle Management Procedure
• Data Anonymization and Pseudonymization Standard
• Privacy Notice
• Breach Notification Procedure
• Records Management Policy

This research focuses on privacy, data protection, data lifecycle, anonymization, breach notification, and records retention requirements.

Official Sources and Requirements

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

Source
European Union
Website: https://eur-lex.europa.eu

Regulation: Regulation (EU) 2016/679

Key Articles and Principles
• Article 5: Principles relating to processing of personal data
• Article 6: Lawfulness of processing
• Article 24: Responsibility of the controller
• Articles 25 and 32: Data protection by design and by default; Security of processing
• Articles 28 and 30: Processor obligations; Records of processing
• Articles 33 and 34: Notification of personal data breaches
• Articles 12–23: Data subject rights
• Chapter V: International transfers

TNDS Compliance
• Data Handling Standard and Privacy Management Policy embed lawfulness, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity and confidentiality.
• Data Lifecycle Management and Records Management Policy define retention and deletion in line with Article 5(1)(e).
• Breach Notification Procedure aligns with Articles 33 and 34 for reporting to authorities and affected individuals.
• Privacy Notice reflects transparency obligations under Articles 13 and 14.

California Consumer Privacy Act and California Privacy Rights Act (CCPA/CPRA)

Source
State of California
Website: https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa

Key Requirements
• Notice at collection
• Right to know, delete, correct, and opt out of sale or sharing
• Sensitive personal information limitations
• Service provider and contractor obligations
• Reasonable security measures

TNDS Compliance
• Privacy Notice and Privacy Management Policy describe rights to access, deletion, correction, and opt-out.
• Data Handling Standard and Records Management Policy support deletion and retention obligations.
• Data Processing Agreement template (Package 7) supports service provider obligations in combination with Data Handling controls.

HIPAA Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules

Source
U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)
Website: https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa

Regulations: 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164

Relevant Requirements
• Uses and disclosures of protected health information (PHI)
• Minimum necessary standard
• Individual rights (access, amendment, accounting)
• Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
• Breach notification requirements and timelines

TNDS Compliance
• Data Handling Standard and Data Anonymization and Pseudonymization Standard support minimum necessary and de-identification.
• Privacy Management Policy and Records Management Policy address PHI handling, retention, and rights.
• Breach Notification Procedure defines incident handling and notification timelines that meet or exceed HIPAA requirements.

Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) Safeguards Rule

Source
Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
Website: https://www.ftc.gov

Relevant Requirements
• Administrative, technical, and physical safeguards for customer information
• Risk assessments and controls aligned to information sensitivity
• Service provider oversight
• Data retention and disposal

TNDS Compliance
• Data Handling Standard mandates appropriate safeguards by data classification.
• Records Management Policy and Data Lifecycle Management govern retention and secure disposition.
• Vendor and DPA templates (Package 7) support service provider oversight.

NIST Privacy Framework and NIST SP 800-122

Source
National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)
Websites: https://www.nist.gov/privacy-framework
; https://csrc.nist.gov

Documents: NIST Privacy Framework; NIST SP 800-122 Guide to Protecting the Confidentiality of Personally Identifiable Information

Key Concepts
• Privacy risk assessment and mitigation
• Data processing inventory and mapping
• Minimization, de-identification, and aggregation
• Safeguards for PII in storage, use, and transmission

TNDS Compliance
• Privacy Management Policy incorporates privacy risk management and inventories.
• Anonymization and Pseudonymization Standard aligns with NIST guidance on de-identification.
• Data Lifecycle Management aligns with NIST storage, use, and disposal guidance.

ISO/IEC 27701 and ISO/IEC 27018

Source
International Organization for Standardization (ISO)
Standards: ISO/IEC 27701 Privacy Information Management; ISO/IEC 27018 Protection of PII in public clouds

Relevant Requirements
• Privacy governance and roles
• PII processing controls for cloud environments
• Processor obligations and transparency

TNDS Compliance
• Privacy Management Policy aligns with a privacy information management system model.
• Cloud-specific privacy controls in Package 5 and Data Handling Standard align with ISO 27018 expectations for cloud-hosted data.
