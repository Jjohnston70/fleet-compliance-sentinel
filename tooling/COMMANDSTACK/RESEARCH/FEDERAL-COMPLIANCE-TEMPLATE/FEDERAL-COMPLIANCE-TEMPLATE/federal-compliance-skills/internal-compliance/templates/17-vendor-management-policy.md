VENDOR AND THIRD-PARTY RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY

Purpose

This Vendor and Third-Party Risk Management Policy establishes [COMPANY_LEGAL_NAME]'s requirements for assessing, managing, and monitoring security and compliance risks associated with vendors, suppliers, contractors, and other third parties. This policy ensures third parties meet security standards and do not introduce unacceptable risk.

Scope

This policy applies to all [COMPANY_NAME] vendors, suppliers, contractors, subcontractors, consultants, and other third parties that access company systems, handle company data, or provide services to the company. It applies to all personnel involved in vendor selection, management, and oversight.

Governance and Cross-Package References

This policy operates under the authority of the Security and Compliance Handbook (security-governance skill), which establishes the master governance framework for third-party risk management (Section 12).

Third-Party Risk Management Principles

Risk-Based Approach
Third-party risk assessed based on access to systems and data
Higher-risk third parties subject to more rigorous assessment
Risk assessment drives security requirements and monitoring
Risk continuously monitored throughout relationship

Due Diligence

Contractual Protection
Security and compliance requirements included in contracts

Ongoing Monitoring
Third-party security and compliance monitored throughout relationship

Third-Party Categories

Critical Third Parties
Access to Restricted or Confidential data
Access to critical systems or infrastructure
Provide critical business services
Examples: Cloud service providers, managed service providers, payroll processors

High-Risk Third Parties
Access to Internal data or systems
Provide important business services
Handle sensitive business information
Examples: Software vendors, consultants, professional services firms

Medium-Risk Third Parties
Limited access to systems or data
Provide standard business services
Minimal security risk
Examples: Office suppliers, marketing vendors, training providers

Low-Risk Third Parties
No access to systems or data
Provide commodity services
Negligible security risk
Examples: Facility services, catering, general suppliers

Third-Party Lifecycle Management

Vendor Selection and Onboarding

Step 1 — Business Need Identification
Business need for third-party service identified
Requirements documented
Budget approved
Vendor selection process initiated

Step 2 — Vendor Identification
Potential vendors identified
Vendor capabilities assessed
Vendor proposals or quotes requested
Vendor shortlist created

Step 3 — Risk Assessment
Vendor risk assessment conducted
Assessment based on data access, system access, service criticality
Security questionnaire sent to vendor
Vendor security practices evaluated
Risk level assigned (Critical, High, Medium, Low)

Step 4 — Due Diligence
Vendor background check conducted
Vendor references contacted
Vendor certifications verified (ISO 27001, SOC 2, etc.)
Vendor financial stability assessed
Due diligence findings documented

Step 5 — Security Review
Vendor security controls reviewed
Vendor security policies and procedures assessed
Vendor incident response capabilities evaluated
Vendor compliance with regulations assessed (GDPR, HIPAA, etc.)
Security review findings documented

Step 6 — Contract Negotiation
Contract terms negotiated
Security and compliance requirements included
Data protection and confidentiality clauses included
Service level agreements (SLAs) defined
Incident notification requirements specified
Right to audit included
Insurance requirements specified
Contract reviewed by legal counsel

Step 7 — Vendor Approval
Vendor approval based on risk assessment, due diligence, and security review
Critical and High-Risk vendors approved by CISO
Vendor approval documented

Step 8 — Vendor Onboarding
Vendor access provisioned per least privilege
Vendor personnel background checks required for Critical vendors
Vendor security training provided if accessing systems or data
Vendor contact information documented
Onboarding documented

Vendor Security Requirements

Contractual Security Requirements

Data Protection
Vendor protects company data per contract and applicable laws
Vendor implements appropriate security controls
Vendor encrypts data in transit and at rest
Vendor does not use data for unauthorized purposes
Vendor does not share data with unauthorized parties

Confidentiality
Vendor maintains confidentiality of company information
Vendor personnel sign confidentiality agreements
Vendor protects trade secrets and proprietary information
Confidentiality obligations survive contract termination

Access Control
Vendor access limited to minimum necessary (least privilege)
Vendor implements strong authentication (passwords, MFA)
Vendor access logged and monitored
Vendor access reviewed regularly

Incident Notification
Vendor notifies company of security incidents within 24 hours
Notification includes incident details, impact, and response actions
Vendor cooperates with company incident response
Vendor provides incident reports and documentation

Compliance
Vendor complies with applicable laws and regulations (GDPR, HIPAA, etc.)
Vendor maintains relevant certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001, etc.)
Vendor provides evidence of compliance upon request
Vendor notifies company of compliance issues

Subcontractors
Vendor obtains company approval before using subcontractors
Subcontractors meet same security requirements as vendor
Vendor responsible for subcontractor security and compliance
Vendor flow-down security requirements to subcontractors

Audit Rights
Company has right to audit vendor security controls
Audits conducted with reasonable notice
Vendor cooperates with audits
Vendor remediates audit findings

Insurance
Vendor maintains appropriate insurance (cyber liability, errors and omissions, general liability)
Insurance coverage amounts specified in contract
Vendor provides certificates of insurance
Company named as additional insured where appropriate

Termination
Contract may be terminated for security breaches or non-compliance
Termination process includes data return and access revocation
Termination obligations survive contract end

Vulnerability Management

Business Continuity
