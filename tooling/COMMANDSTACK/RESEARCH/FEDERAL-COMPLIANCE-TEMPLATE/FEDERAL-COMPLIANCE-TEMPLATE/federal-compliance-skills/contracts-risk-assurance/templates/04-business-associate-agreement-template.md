BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AGREEMENT TEMPLATE

Purpose

This Business Associate Agreement (BAA) Template provides [COMPANY_LEGAL_NAME] with a standardized legal framework for handling Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of HIPAA-covered entities. This template ensures compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health (HITECH) Act.

Scope

This template applies to all [COMPANY_NAME] client engagements where [COMPANY_NAME] creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of a covered entity. It applies to all personnel involved in PHI handling activities.

This BAA governs Business Associate's use and disclosure of Protected Health Information (PHI) in connection with services provided under the Agreement.

1. Definitions

1.1 General Definitions
Terms used but not defined in this BAA shall have the meanings set forth in the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA"), the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act"), and their implementing regulations, including the Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E) and the Security Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and C).

1.2 Specific Definitions
"Protected Health Information" or "PHI" means individually identifiable health information that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity.

"Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI" means PHI that is transmitted by or maintained in electronic media.

"Security Incident" means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system.

"Breach" means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the Privacy Rule that compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.

2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures of PHI

2.1 Services
Business Associate may use and disclose PHI only as necessary to perform services specified in the Agreement, as permitted by this BAA, and as required by law.

2.2 Business Associate's Operations
Business Associate may use PHI for its proper management and administration or to carry out its legal responsibilities, provided such use is permitted under the Privacy Rule.

2.3 Disclosure for Business Associate's Operations
Business Associate may disclose PHI for its proper management and administration or to carry out its legal responsibilities, provided:
Disclosure is required by law, or
Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient that the PHI will be held confidentially and used or further disclosed only as required by law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed, and the recipient will notify Business Associate of any Breach of which it becomes aware.

2.4 Data Aggregation
Business Associate may use PHI to provide data aggregation services to Covered Entity as permitted by the Privacy Rule.

2.5 De-identified Information
Business Associate may de-identify PHI in accordance with the Privacy Rule and use and disclose such de-identified information without restriction.

3. Business Associate Obligations

3.1 Compliance with HIPAA
Business Associate shall comply with the applicable requirements of HIPAA, the HITECH Act, and their implementing regulations in its use and disclosure of PHI.

3.2 Prohibited Uses and Disclosures
Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI except as permitted or required by this BAA or as required by law.

3.3 Minimum Necessary
Business Associate shall use, disclose, and request only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose of the use, disclosure, or request.

3.4 Safeguards
Business Associate shall implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of PHI other than as provided by this BAA and to comply with the Security Rule with respect to ePHI.

3.5 Safeguards Detail
Safeguards include:
Administrative Safeguards: Security management process, workforce security, information access management, security awareness and training, security incident procedures, contingency plan, evaluation
Physical Safeguards: Facility access controls, workstation use and security, device and media controls
Technical Safeguards: Access control, audit controls, integrity controls, transmission security

3.6 Reporting
Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity:
Any use or disclosure of PHI not provided for by this BAA within [5] business days of becoming aware
Any Security Incident of which it becomes aware within [24] hours
Any Breach of unsecured PHI without unreasonable delay and in no case later than [10] business days after discovery

3.7 Breach Notification Content
Breach notification shall include:
Identification of each individual whose unsecured PHI has been or is reasonably believed to have been accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed
Brief description of what happened, including date of Breach and date of discovery
Description of types of unsecured PHI involved
Steps individuals should take to protect themselves
Brief description of what Business Associate is doing to investigate, mitigate harm, and prevent further Breaches
Contact information for individuals to ask questions

3.8 Mitigation
Business Associate shall mitigate, to the extent practicable, any harmful effect of a use or disclosure of PHI by Business Associate in violation of this BAA.

3.9 Subcontractors
Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of Business Associate agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate with respect to such PHI.

3.10 Access to PHI
Business Associate shall provide access to PHI in a Designated Record Set to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an individual, within [10] business days of request, in the time and manner designated by Covered Entity, to meet Covered Entity's obligations under the Privacy Rule.

3.11 Amendment of PHI
Business Associate shall make PHI in a Designated Record Set available to Covered Entity for amendment and incorporate any amendments to PHI within [10] business days of request, in the time and manner designated by Covered Entity, to meet Covered Entity's obligations under the Privacy Rule.

3.12 Accounting of Disclosures
Business Associate shall document disclosures of PHI and information related to such disclosures as would be required for Covered Entity to respond to a request by an individual for an accounting of disclosures in accordance with the Privacy Rule. Business Associate shall provide such information to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an individual within [10] business days of request.

3.13 Internal Practices
Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services for purposes of determining Covered Entity's compliance with HIPAA.

3.14 Policies and Procedures
Business Associate shall maintain written policies and procedures regarding PHI that comply with HIPAA and the HITECH Act.

3.15 Training
Business Associate shall train workforce members on HIPAA requirements and Business Associate's policies and procedures regarding PHI.

3.16 Sanctions
Business Associate shall apply appropriate sanctions against workforce members who fail to comply with HIPAA requirements or Business Associate's policies and procedures regarding PHI.

4. Covered Entity Obligations

4.1 Permissible Requests
Covered Entity shall not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would not be permissible under the Privacy Rule if done by Covered Entity.

4.2 Notice of Privacy Practices
Covered Entity shall provide Business Associate with a copy of its Notice of Privacy Practices and any changes thereto.

4.3 Restrictions
Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any restriction on the use or disclosure of PHI that Covered Entity has agreed to in accordance with the Privacy Rule.

4.4 Revocations
Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any revocation of authorization by an individual to use or disclose PHI.

5. Term and Termination

5.1 Term
This BAA shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for the term of the Agreement.

5.2 Termination for Breach
Upon Covered Entity's knowledge of a material breach by Business Associate, Covered Entity shall:
Provide an opportunity for Business Associate to cure the breach or end the violation and terminate this BAA and the Agreement if Business Associate does not cure the breach or end the violation within [30] days, or
Immediately terminate this BAA and the Agreement if cure is not possible.

5.3 Effect of Termination
Upon termination of this BAA, Business Associate shall:
Return or destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity that Business Associate maintains in any form
Retain no copies of PHI
If return or destruction is not feasible, extend the protections of this BAA to such PHI and limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make the return or destruction infeasible

5.4 Survival
Obligations relating to return or destruction of PHI and confidentiality shall survive termination of this BAA.

6. Liability and Indemnification

6.1 Liability
Business Associate shall be liable for civil and criminal penalties imposed on Covered Entity or Business Associate for violations of HIPAA or the HITECH Act resulting from Business Associate's breach of this BAA.

6.2 Indemnification
Business Associate shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Covered Entity from and against all claims, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from or relating to Business Associate's breach of this BAA or violation of HIPAA or the HITECH Act.

7. General Provisions

7.1 Relationship to Agreement
This BAA forms part of the Agreement. In the event of conflict between this BAA and the Agreement with respect to PHI, this BAA shall control.

7.2 Amendments
This BAA may be amended by written agreement of the Parties. The Parties agree to negotiate in good faith to amend this BAA to comply with changes in HIPAA, the HITECH Act, or their implementing regulations.

7.3 Interpretation
Any ambiguity in this BAA shall be resolved in favor of a meaning that permits Covered Entity to comply with HIPAA and the HITECH Act.

7.4 Regulatory References
References to regulatory provisions shall be to such provisions as they may be amended from time to time.

7.5 Governing Law
This BAA shall be governed by the same law as the Agreement, except to the extent preempted by federal law.

7.6 Severability
If any provision of this BAA is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.
