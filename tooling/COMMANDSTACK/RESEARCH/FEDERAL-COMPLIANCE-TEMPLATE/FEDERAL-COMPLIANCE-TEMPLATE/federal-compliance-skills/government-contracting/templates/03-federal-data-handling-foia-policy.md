FEDERAL DATA HANDLING AND FOIA POLICY

Purpose

This Federal Data Handling and FOIA Policy establishes [COMPANY_NAME]'s procedures for handling federal government data, maintaining records in compliance with federal requirements, and responding to Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests.

Scope

This policy applies to all [COMPANY_NAME] personnel, contractors, and subcontractors who handle federal government data or records subject to FOIA requirements. This policy covers data classification, handling procedures, records management, disclosure requirements, and FOIA response procedures.

Federal Data Classification

Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)
Definition: Information that requires safeguarding or dissemination controls pursuant to federal law, regulation, or government-wide policy
Handling: Stored in approved systems with access controls and encryption
Marking: All CUI documents marked with appropriate CUI markings
Transmission: Encrypted transmission methods only
Disposal: Secure destruction methods with documented disposal

Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
Definition: Information that can be used to distinguish or trace an individual's identity
Examples: Social Security Numbers, dates of birth, biometric data, financial account numbers
Handling: Minimum necessary access, encryption at rest and in transit
Storage: Segregated storage with enhanced access controls
Breach Response: Immediate notification per federal breach notification requirements

Protected Health Information (PHI)
Definition: Health information subject to HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules
Handling: HIPAA-compliant safeguards and business associate agreements
Access: Limited to authorized personnel with documented need-to-know
Transmission: HIPAA-compliant secure transmission methods
Retention: Maintained per HIPAA retention requirements

Federal Tax Information (FTI)
Definition: Tax return information subject to IRC Section 6103
Handling: IRS Publication 1075 safeguards and controls
Access: Background-checked personnel with signed confidentiality agreements
Storage: Physically and logically separated from other data
Audit: Annual IRS safeguard reviews and audits

Classified Information
Definition: Information classified as Confidential, Secret, or Top Secret
Handling: Only with appropriate facility clearance and personnel clearances
Storage: Approved classified information storage facilities
Access: Cleared personnel with documented need-to-know
Transmission: Approved classified communication channels only

Unclassified Federal Data
Definition: Federal government data not subject to special handling requirements
Handling: Standard security controls and access management
Storage: Secure systems with appropriate access controls
Transmission: Encrypted transmission methods preferred

Federal Data Handling Procedures

Data Receipt and Intake
All federal data received is logged and classified upon receipt
Data classification markings reviewed and applied
Access permissions configured based on classification and need-to-know
Data owner and custodian assigned for each federal dataset

Data Storage
Federal data stored in systems meeting federal security requirements
Encryption at rest for all federal data
Logical separation of federal data from commercial data
Access controls enforced through role-based access control (RBAC)
Regular access reviews and recertification

Data Access
Access granted based on need-to-know and least-privilege principles
Multi-factor authentication required for all federal data access
Access requests documented and approved by data owner
Access logs maintained for all federal data systems
Privileged access monitored and audited

Data Transmission
Federal data transmitted only through encrypted channels
Secure file transfer protocols (SFTP, HTTPS, encrypted email)
Transmission logs maintained for audit purposes
Recipient verification required before transmission
Encryption keys managed per federal key management requirements

Data Use
Federal data used only for authorized contract purposes
Prohibition on unauthorized disclosure or use
Data minimization principles applied
Purpose limitation enforced through technical and administrative controls
Regular monitoring for unauthorized use or access

Data Retention
Federal data retained per contract requirements and federal records schedules
Retention periods documented in records retention schedule
Automated retention enforcement where feasible
Legal holds applied when required
Retention compliance monitored and audited

Data Disposal
Secure disposal methods for all federal data
Media sanitization per NIST SP 800-88 guidelines
Certificates of destruction maintained for audit purposes
Disposal logs documenting date, method, and personnel
Verification of complete data destruction

Records Management

Federal Records Definition
Records created or received in the course of federal contract performance
Records documenting contract deliverables and performance
Records required by contract terms or federal regulations
Records subject to federal records retention requirements

Records Retention Schedule
Contract records retained per Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) requirements
Financial records retained for minimum of 3 years after final payment
Personnel records retained per federal requirements
Technical documentation retained per contract terms
Audit records retained for minimum of 3 years or per contract requirements

Records Storage
Electronic records stored in secure, backed-up systems
Physical records stored in secure, access-controlled facilities
Records indexed and retrievable for audit or FOIA purposes
Backup copies maintained in geographically separate location
Records protected from unauthorized access, alteration, or destruction

Records Disposition
Records disposed of only after retention period expires
Disposition approval obtained from contracting officer when required
Secure destruction methods for all federal records
Disposition documentation maintained per federal requirements
Legal holds honored and disposition suspended when required

Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) Compliance

FOIA Applicability
[COMPANY_NAME] recognizes that federal agency records may be subject to FOIA
Records created or maintained on behalf of federal agencies may be subject to disclosure
Contractor-generated records may be deemed agency records subject to FOIA
Proprietary and confidential business information may be protected from disclosure

FOIA Request Procedures

Step 1 — FOIA Request Receipt
All FOIA requests forwarded immediately to designated FOIA Coordinator
FOIA request logged with date, requester information, and scope
Contracting Officer's Representative (COR) notified within 24 hours
Legal counsel consulted as needed

Step 2 — Records Search and Review
Comprehensive search conducted for responsive records
Records reviewed for responsiveness to FOIA request
Exemptions and exclusions identified and documented
Privileged or protected information identified

Step 3 — Agency Coordination
Federal agency contracting officer notified of FOIA request
Records provided to agency for review and determination
Agency guidance followed for disclosure decisions
Contractor recommendations provided regarding proprietary information

Step 4 — Response Preparation
Responsive records compiled and organized
Redactions applied per agency direction
Exemption justifications documented
Response package prepared for agency review

Step 5 — Response Delivery
Response delivered to federal agency within required timeframe
Copy of response maintained in FOIA response log
Follow-up coordination with agency as needed
Lessons learned documented for future requests
