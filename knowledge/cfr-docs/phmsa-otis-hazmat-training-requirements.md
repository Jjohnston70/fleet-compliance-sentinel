# PHMSA Hazardous Materials Training Requirements (OTIS Platform)

## Overview

The U.S. Department of Transportation's Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration (PHMSA) mandates hazardous materials training for all hazmat employees under 49 CFR Part 172, Subpart H. Training is delivered through the OTIS platform (HSI Platform by OSM), which provides SCORM-based interactive courses with built-in tracking, transcripts, and certificate generation.

This document covers the required training modules, supplemental courses, credit pathways, and compliance tracking requirements relevant to fleet operators handling hazardous materials.

---

## Required Training Modules

PHMSA requires completion of 12 core training modules covering the full scope of hazardous materials regulations. These modules align directly with the Emergency Response Guidebook (ERG) structure and 49 CFR Part 172-180 requirements.

### Module 0.0 - Hazardous Materials Regulations Introduction

Foundation module covering the regulatory framework for hazmat transportation. Includes overview of 49 CFR Parts 171-180, roles and responsibilities of hazmat employees, and enforcement mechanisms.

### Module 1.0 - Hazardous Materials Table

Covers the Hazardous Materials Table (49 CFR 172.101), including proper shipping names, hazard classes, identification numbers, packing groups, and special provisions. This is the primary lookup reference for all hazmat classification decisions.

### Module 2.0 - Shipping Papers

Requirements for hazmat shipping documentation under 49 CFR 172.200-205. Covers required information, emergency response telephone numbers, shipper certifications, and retention requirements.

### Module 3.0 - Packaging

Packaging requirements under 49 CFR Parts 173 and 178. Covers performance-oriented packaging standards, UN specification packaging, authorized packagings by hazard class, and quantity limitations.

### Module 4.0 - Marking

Marking requirements under 49 CFR 172.300-338. Covers proper shipping name marking, UN identification number marking, special markings (ORM-D, limited quantity, marine pollutant), and marking specifications.

### Module 5.0 - Labeling

Labeling requirements under 49 CFR 172.400-450. Covers hazard class labels, subsidiary hazard labels, label placement, and the relationship between labels and placards.

### Module 6.0 - Placarding

Placarding requirements under 49 CFR 172.500-560. Covers Table 1 and Table 2 placards, placarding exceptions, DANGEROUS placard usage, and placard specifications and placement.

### Module 7.0a - Carrier Requirements: Highway

Highway-specific carrier requirements under 49 CFR Parts 177 and 397. Covers loading and unloading rules, segregation requirements, routing restrictions, attendance requirements, and incident reporting for motor carriers.

### Module 7.0b - Carrier Requirements: Air

Air transport requirements under 49 CFR Part 175 and ICAO Technical Instructions. Covers quantity limitations for passenger vs cargo aircraft, accessibility requirements, and air-specific packaging requirements.

### Module 7.0c - Carrier Requirements: Rail

Rail transport requirements under 49 CFR Part 174. Covers train placement rules, speed restrictions, switching operations, and rail-specific incident response procedures.

### Module 7.0d - Carrier Requirements: Vessel

Vessel transport requirements under 49 CFR Part 176 and IMDG Code. Covers stowage requirements, segregation on vessels, and vessel-specific documentation requirements.

### Module 8.0 - Security Requirements

Security plan requirements under 49 CFR 172.800-804. Covers security plan development, personnel security, en route security, and security awareness training requirements for hazmat employees.

## Training Compliance Requirements

### Recurrence and Deadlines

Hazmat training must be completed within 90 days of employment or change in job function (49 CFR 172.704(c)). Recurrent training is required at least once every three years. The current OTIS training window runs from April 1, 2026 through February 14, 2027.

### Dual Credit Pathways

Each required module offers two completion pathways:

- **SCORM Online Course**: Interactive browser-based module with embedded assessments. This is the primary completion method tracked by OTIS.
- **General Task Equivalent**: Alternative credit path for employees who complete equivalent training through other approved methods (classroom instruction, hands-on demonstration, etc.).

Both pathways generate completion records in the OTIS transcript. For compliance tracking purposes, document which pathway each employee used.

### Record Retention

Training records must be retained for the duration of employment plus 90 days after the employee leaves hazmat duties (49 CFR 172.704(d)). Records must include: employee name, completion date, training materials used, name and address of trainer, and certification that training was completed.

### Certificates

OTIS generates PDF certificates upon course completion. These appear in the Transcript tab and can be downloaded for compliance files. No certificates are generated until a course is marked complete.

## Supplemental Training Courses

OTIS provides 19 additional courses outside the required training plan, available indefinitely through December 31, 2199. These are optional but valuable for building deeper hazmat response competency.

### NFPA 472 Awareness Series (6 Units)

The NFPA 472 Awareness series covers the competency requirements for first responders at the awareness level. These align with NFPA 472: Standard for Competence of Responders to Hazardous Materials/Weapons of Mass Destruction Incidents.

- **Unit 1 - Introduction**: Overview of hazmat/WMD incident response framework and responder roles.
- **Unit 2 - Anatomy of a HM-WMD Response**: Incident phases, response structure, and the incident command system as applied to hazmat events.
- **Unit 3 - Recognition and Identification of HM**: Methods for identifying hazardous materials including placards, labels, container shapes, shipping papers, and SDS.
- **Unit 4 - HM Transportation Classifications and Hazards**: The nine DOT hazard classes, division system, and associated health and physical hazards.
- **Unit 5 - Using the USDOT ERG**: How to use the Emergency Response Guidebook for initial response actions, including the yellow/blue/orange page system and protective distance tables.
- **Unit 6 - Protective Actions**: Isolation, evacuation, and shelter-in-place procedures. Includes initial isolation distances and protective action distances from the ERG green pages.

### Operations Series (12 Units)

The Operations series covers NFPA 472 competencies for first responders at the operations level -- a higher level of hazmat response capability.

- **Unit 01 - Responsibilities of the First Responder**: Legal duties, scene safety, and the role of operations-level responders within incident command.
- **Unit 02 - Toxicology and Physical Hazards**: Exposure routes, dose-response relationships, exposure limits (TLV, PEL, IDLH), and acute vs chronic health effects.
- **Unit 03 - Physical and Chemical Properties**: Flash point, boiling point, vapor density, specific gravity, solubility, and how these properties affect hazmat behavior and response.
- **Unit 04 - Collecting and Interpreting Response Information**: Using SDS, CHEMTREC, ERG, NIOSH Pocket Guide, and other reference sources during incidents.
- **Unit 05 - Introduction to Containers**: Container design principles, pressure ratings, stress types, and how container failure occurs.
- **Unit 06 - Highway Containers**: Cargo tanks (MC/DOT specifications), portable tanks, intermodal containers, and non-bulk packaging used in highway transportation.
- **Unit 07 - Rail and Intermodal Transportation Containers**: Tank cars (DOT specifications), covered hopper cars, intermodal tanks, and rail-specific container identification.
- **Unit 08 - Non-Bulk Containers**: Drums, cylinders, bags, carboys, jerricans, and other non-bulk packaging. Includes UN performance packaging standards.
- **Unit 09 - Fixed Storage Containers and Pipeline**: Atmospheric storage tanks, pressurized storage vessels, cryogenic containers, and pipeline systems.
- **Unit 10 - Personal Protective Equipment**: PPE levels (A through D), chemical protective clothing, respiratory protection, and PPE selection based on hazard assessment.
- **Unit 11 - Decontamination**: Decontamination principles, emergency decon procedures, technical decon, and mass casualty decontamination.
- **Unit 12 - Evaluating the Response**: Post-incident analysis, critique procedures, documentation requirements, and lessons learned.

### Additional Resources

- **PHMSA Hazmat Grant Program Overview**: Information on PHMSA's grant programs for hazmat training and emergency preparedness planning, available to state, local, and tribal governments.

## Platform Technical Details

### OTIS Platform (HSI by OSM)

- **URL**: https://otis.osmanager4.com
- **Platform Provider**: HSI (Health & Safety Institute)
- **Administering Agency**: PHMSA (Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration)
- **Course Format**: SCORM (Sharable Content Object Reference Model) -- browser-based interactive modules
- **Support**: 1-800-447-3177, Monday-Friday 9AM-7PM Eastern

### Tracking Capabilities

The OTIS platform tracks: enrollment status (not started, in progress, complete, delinquent), completion dates, certificate generation, training window compliance, and transcript history. These data points should map to FCS compliance tracking fields.

## Relevance to Fleet Compliance Sentinel

### Compliance Tracking Integration Points

FCS should track the following per employee for hazmat training compliance:

- **Training window status**: Whether the employee is within an active training window
- **Module completion status**: Which of the 12 required modules are complete vs outstanding
- **Credit pathway used**: SCORM online vs General Task equivalent
- **Completion dates**: Per module, for recurrence tracking (3-year cycle)
- **Certificate status**: Whether PDF certificates have been downloaded and filed
- **Supplemental training**: Optional NFPA 472 course completion for enhanced qualifications
- **Delinquency alerts**: Flag employees approaching or past due dates

### Data Sources

Training completion data can be sourced from: OTIS transcript exports, manual entry from supervisors, or future API integration if OTIS/HSI exposes one. Currently, the most reliable method is certificate PDF downloads combined with manual tracking in the FCS employee compliance module.
