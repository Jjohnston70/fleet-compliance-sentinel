# FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse — Employer Requirements

## Section 1: What the Clearinghouse Is

The FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse is a secure, online federal database that contains records of drug and alcohol program violations for commercial driver's license (CDL) and commercial learner's permit (CLP) holders. The Clearinghouse is operated by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) under 49 CFR Part 382, Subpart G.

The Clearinghouse became mandatory for all FMCSA-regulated employers on January 6, 2020 (49 CFR §382.701). During the initial three-year phase-in period (January 6, 2020 through January 5, 2023), employers could still use the traditional manual process of contacting prior employers for drug and alcohol testing history under 49 CFR §391.23(e). That phase-in ended on January 6, 2023, at which point full Clearinghouse queries became the mandatory method for obtaining a driver's violation history from FMCSA-regulated prior employers (49 CFR §391.23(e)(4)).

The Clearinghouse serves a single purpose: to give employers, FMCSA, and state licensing agencies real-time access to information about CDL/CLP driver drug and alcohol violations, so that drivers who are prohibited from operating a commercial motor vehicle (CMV) cannot conceal that status by moving to a new employer.

**Key regulatory authority:**

- Establishment and operation: 49 CFR Part 382, Subpart G (§§382.700–382.727)
- Drug and alcohol testing requirements: 49 CFR Part 382, Subpart B
- Testing procedures: 49 CFR Part 40
- Pre-employment investigation: 49 CFR §391.23

## Section 2: Employer Registration Requirements

Every employer who employs CDL/CLP holders subject to FMCSA drug and alcohol testing regulations must register with the Clearinghouse (49 CFR §382.701(a)). Registration is completed at clearinghouse.fmcsa.dot.gov using the employer's USDOT number.

Employers may designate a consortium/third-party administrator (C/TPA) to conduct Clearinghouse queries on their behalf (49 CFR §382.711). The C/TPA must also register with the Clearinghouse and must be authorized by the employer before conducting queries. Even when a C/TPA conducts queries, the employer retains ultimate responsibility for compliance with all Clearinghouse requirements (49 CFR §382.711(b)).

Registration types:

- **Employer registration:** Required for all FMCSA-regulated employers. Uses USDOT number. Grants ability to conduct queries and report violations directly.
- **C/TPA registration:** Optional delegation. C/TPA registers separately, then employer links the C/TPA to their account. C/TPA may query and report on the employer's behalf.

Failure to register does not exempt an employer from Clearinghouse requirements. An unregistered employer is still obligated to query the Clearinghouse and is subject to penalties for non-compliance (49 CFR §386.72).

## Section 3: Pre-Employment Query Requirements

Before permitting any CDL/CLP holder to operate a CMV for the first time, the employer must conduct a full query of the Clearinghouse (49 CFR §382.701(b)(1)). This applies to every new hire and to any driver who has not previously been queried by the employer.

**Consent requirement:** The employer must obtain the driver's written or electronic consent before conducting a full query (49 CFR §382.701(b)). Electronic consent is provided through the Clearinghouse system — the driver must log in to the Clearinghouse and grant consent electronically.

**If the driver refuses consent:** The employer must not permit the driver to perform safety-sensitive functions, including operating a CMV (49 CFR §382.701(b)(3)). There is no exception or workaround. A driver who refuses consent cannot drive.

**January 6, 2023 transition:** As of January 6, 2023, a full Clearinghouse query satisfies the requirement to investigate a driver's drug and alcohol testing history with FMCSA-regulated prior employers (49 CFR §391.23(e)(4)). Employers are no longer required to manually contact prior FMCSA-regulated employers for this information. However, employers must still contact prior employers who are not FMCSA-regulated (e.g., employers regulated by FAA, FRA, FTA, PHMSA, or USCG) using the traditional manual inquiry process (49 CFR §391.23(e)).

**What a full query returns:** A full query returns the complete record of any drug and alcohol program violations associated with the driver, including the violation type, date, employer who reported it, and whether the driver has completed the return-to-duty process.

## Section 4: Annual Query Requirements

Employers must conduct a limited query of the Clearinghouse at least once every 12 months for each CDL/CLP holder they employ (49 CFR §382.701(b)(2)).

**Limited query vs. full query:**

- **Limited query:** Does not require individual driver consent through the Clearinghouse (but does require a general consent form — see below). A limited query returns only whether the driver has any records in the Clearinghouse. It does not reveal the details of any violations.
- **Full query:** Required when a limited query indicates the driver has a record. A full query requires the driver's electronic consent through the Clearinghouse and returns the complete violation record.

**Consent for limited queries:** Employers must obtain a general consent from the driver before conducting limited queries (49 CFR §382.701(b)(2)). This consent may cover an indefinite period of employment and may be obtained on paper or electronically. It is separate from the electronic consent required for full queries.

**Workflow when a limited query returns a match:**

1. Employer conducts annual limited query — result indicates driver has a record.
2. Employer must promptly request a full query through the Clearinghouse.
3. Driver must log in and provide electronic consent for the full query.
4. If the full query reveals a prohibited status, the employer must immediately remove the driver from safety-sensitive functions (49 CFR §382.211).
5. If the driver refuses to provide consent for the full query, the employer must not permit the driver to operate a CMV (49 CFR §382.701(b)(3)).

**Documentation:** Employers must document all Clearinghouse queries, including the date of the query, the type of query (limited or full), and the result. This documentation must be retained as part of the driver qualification file.

## Section 5: What "Prohibited" Status Means

A driver has "prohibited" status when the driver is prohibited from performing safety-sensitive functions under 49 CFR Part 382. Safety-sensitive functions include operating a CMV, as well as all time spent inspecting, servicing, or conditioning any CMV; time spent at the driving controls of a CMV; all time loading or unloading a CMV; and all time repairing, obtaining assistance, or remaining in attendance upon a disabled CMV (49 CFR §382.107).

**Causes of prohibited status:**

- A verified positive drug test result (49 CFR §382.201)
- An alcohol test result of 0.04 or greater (49 CFR §382.201)
- Refusal to submit to a required drug or alcohol test (49 CFR §382.211)
- Any other violation under 49 CFR Part 382, Subpart B (49 CFR §382.201–§382.215)
- An actual knowledge determination by the employer that the driver used alcohol or controlled substances in violation of the regulations (49 CFR §382.205, §382.207, §382.209, §382.213, §382.215)

**Employer obligations upon discovering prohibited status:**

The employer must immediately remove the driver from all safety-sensitive functions (49 CFR §382.211). The employer must not permit the driver to operate a CMV or perform any other safety-sensitive function until the driver has completed the return-to-duty process (49 CFR §382.605(a)).

There is no grace period. There is no discretionary allowance for the employer to delay removal. Once the employer has actual knowledge or receives Clearinghouse information indicating a prohibited status, immediate removal is required (49 CFR §382.211).

## Section 6: Employer Reporting Obligations

Employers must report the following events to the Clearinghouse (49 CFR §382.705(b)):

1. **Positive drug test results conducted under DOT authority** — reported by the Medical Review Officer (MRO) directly to the Clearinghouse (49 CFR §382.705(a)). The employer does not report positive drug test results — the MRO does.

2. **Alcohol test results of 0.04 or greater** — the employer must report to the Clearinghouse within 3 business days of learning of the result (49 CFR §382.705(b)(1)).

3. **Refusals to test** — the employer must report to the Clearinghouse within 3 business days of the refusal (49 CFR §382.705(b)(2)). Refusal includes failure to appear for a test, failure to remain at the testing site, failure to provide a specimen, failure to permit a directly observed collection when required, and other conduct defined in 49 CFR §40.191 and §40.261.

4. **Actual knowledge violations** — when an employer makes an actual knowledge determination that a driver used alcohol or controlled substances in violation of 49 CFR Part 382, the employer must report to the Clearinghouse within 3 business days (49 CFR §382.705(b)(3)).

5. **Negative return-to-duty test results** — the employer (or C/TPA) must report the negative return-to-duty test result to the Clearinghouse (49 CFR §382.705(b)(4)).

6. **Completion of follow-up testing plan** — when the driver has completed the follow-up testing program prescribed by the substance abuse professional (SAP), the employer must report completion to the Clearinghouse (49 CFR §382.705(b)(5)).

**Reporting deadline:** All employer-reported items must be reported within 3 business days of the date the employer learns of the information (49 CFR §382.705(b)).

**Who reports what:**

| Event | Reported By | Citation |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Positive drug test | MRO | 49 CFR §382.705(a) |
| Alcohol test ≥ 0.04 | Employer | 49 CFR §382.705(b)(1) |
| Refusal to test | Employer | 49 CFR §382.705(b)(2) |
| Actual knowledge violation | Employer | 49 CFR §382.705(b)(3) |
| Negative RTD test | Employer/C/TPA | 49 CFR §382.705(b)(4) |
| Follow-up testing complete | Employer | 49 CFR §382.705(b)(5) |

## Section 7: Return-to-Duty (RTD) Requirements

A driver who has been determined to have violated 49 CFR Part 382, Subpart B must complete the full return-to-duty (RTD) process before the employer may permit the driver to resume safety-sensitive functions (49 CFR §382.605).

**RTD process steps:**

1. **SAP evaluation:** The driver must be evaluated by a substance abuse professional (SAP) who meets the qualifications in 49 CFR §40.281 (49 CFR §382.605(b)).

2. **SAP-prescribed education and/or treatment:** The SAP determines what education and/or treatment the driver must complete (49 CFR Part 40, Subpart O).

3. **SAP follow-up evaluation:** After completing the prescribed education/treatment, the driver must return to the SAP for a follow-up evaluation to determine if the driver has demonstrated successful compliance (49 CFR §40.301).

4. **Return-to-duty test:** The driver must pass a return-to-duty drug and/or alcohol test with a verified negative result (49 CFR §382.605(c)(1)). For a drug violation, a negative drug test is required. For an alcohol violation, an alcohol test result below 0.02 is required.

5. **Clearinghouse confirmation:** The negative return-to-duty test result must be reported to the Clearinghouse (49 CFR §382.705(b)(4)). The employer should verify the Clearinghouse reflects the updated status before permitting the driver to return to safety-sensitive duty.

**Follow-up testing after RTD:**

Once the driver returns to duty, the SAP must prescribe a follow-up testing plan of at least 6 unannounced tests in the first 12 months following the driver's return to safety-sensitive functions (49 CFR §382.605(c)(2)). The SAP may require follow-up testing for up to 60 months total. The employer must implement the follow-up testing plan as prescribed by the SAP.

**Employer responsibility:** The employer is not required to hold a position open for a driver undergoing the RTD process. However, if the employer does permit the driver to return to work, the employer must ensure the full RTD process has been completed and documented before allowing the driver to perform safety-sensitive functions (49 CFR §382.605(a)).

## Section 8: Record Retention

Employers must retain records of Clearinghouse queries, consent forms, and related documentation (49 CFR §382.401).

**Retention periods:**

- **Query results and consent forms:** Employers must retain records of all Clearinghouse queries and driver consent forms for a minimum of 3 years from the date of the query (49 CFR §382.401(b)).
- **Clearinghouse system retention:** The Clearinghouse retains employer query history for 5 years from the date of the query. Violation records are retained in the Clearinghouse for 5 years from the date of the final action in the violation record (e.g., completion of the RTD process) (49 CFR §382.717).
- **Driver violation records:** If the driver does not complete the RTD process, the violation record remains in the Clearinghouse indefinitely until the RTD process is completed or until 5 years after the date of the violation determination, whichever occurs first (49 CFR §382.717(a)).

**What to retain:**

- Date and type (limited or full) of each Clearinghouse query
- Query results (including "no records found" results)
- Driver consent forms (written or electronic) for limited and full queries
- Any documentation of driver refusal to provide consent
- Records of violations reported to the Clearinghouse
- RTD documentation including SAP reports and follow-up testing plans

## Section 9: Penalties for Non-Compliance

Employers who fail to comply with Clearinghouse requirements are subject to civil penalties under 49 CFR Part 386 and 49 U.S.C. §31310.

**Penalty amounts (adjusted annually for inflation):**

- Failure to conduct a required pre-employment full query: up to $16,864 per violation per day (49 CFR §386.72)
- Failure to conduct required annual limited query: up to $16,864 per violation per day (49 CFR §386.72)
- Permitting a driver with prohibited status to perform safety-sensitive functions: up to $16,864 per violation per day (49 CFR §386.72)
- Failure to report a violation to the Clearinghouse within the required timeframe: up to $16,864 per violation per day (49 CFR §386.72)
- Using a driver who has refused to provide consent for a required query: up to $16,864 per violation per day (49 CFR §386.72)

**Note:** Penalty amounts are adjusted periodically. The amounts listed above reflect 2024 adjusted values. Check the FMCSA website or 49 CFR §386.72 for the most current penalty amounts.

In addition to civil penalties, patterns of non-compliance may result in compliance reviews, unsatisfactory safety ratings, and federal out-of-service orders that can shut down a carrier's operations entirely.

## Section 10: Practical Employer Checklist

Use this checklist to verify your Clearinghouse compliance:

1. **Register** at clearinghouse.fmcsa.dot.gov using your USDOT number, if not already registered.
2. **Obtain driver consent** (written or electronic) before conducting any Clearinghouse query.
3. **Run a full query** before permitting any new CDL/CLP hire to operate a CMV for the first time (49 CFR §382.701(b)(1)).
4. **Run a limited query** at least once every 12 months for each currently employed CDL/CLP driver (49 CFR §382.701(b)(2)).
5. **If a limited query shows a record** — immediately request a full query with the driver's electronic consent.
6. **If the driver refuses consent** — do not permit the driver to operate a CMV (49 CFR §382.701(b)(3)).
7. **If prohibited status is found** — remove the driver from all safety-sensitive functions immediately (49 CFR §382.211).
8. **Report violations** to the Clearinghouse within 3 business days of occurrence (49 CFR §382.705(b)).
9. **Document all queries** and retain consent records for a minimum of 3 years (49 CFR §382.401(b)).
10. **Before allowing RTD** — confirm the driver has completed the full RTD process and the Clearinghouse reflects a negative return-to-duty test result (49 CFR §382.605).

## Key Terms Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **CDL** | Commercial Driver's License — required to operate commercial motor vehicles as defined in 49 CFR §383.5. |
| **CLP** | Commercial Learner's Permit — a permit issued to an individual for purposes of learning to operate a CMV (49 CFR §383.5). |
| **CMV** | Commercial Motor Vehicle — a vehicle used in commerce to transport passengers or property that meets the weight, passenger, or hazmat thresholds in 49 CFR §382.107. |
| **Safety-sensitive function** | All time from when a driver begins work or is required to be ready to work until relieved from work, including driving, inspecting, servicing, loading/unloading, and attending to a disabled CMV (49 CFR §382.107). |
| **Prohibited status** | A Clearinghouse status indicating the driver has an unresolved drug or alcohol violation and is prohibited from performing safety-sensitive functions (49 CFR §382.211). |
| **Full query** | A Clearinghouse query that returns the complete violation record for a driver. Requires the driver's electronic consent through the Clearinghouse system (49 CFR §382.701(b)). |
| **Limited query** | A Clearinghouse query that returns only whether a driver has any records. Does not require electronic consent but does require general written consent (49 CFR §382.701(b)(2)). |
| **SAP** | Substance Abuse Professional — a qualified professional who evaluates drivers who have violated drug and alcohol regulations and prescribes education, treatment, and follow-up testing requirements (49 CFR §40.281). |
| **RTD** | Return-to-Duty — the process a driver must complete after a drug or alcohol violation before being permitted to resume safety-sensitive functions (49 CFR §382.605). |
| **MRO** | Medical Review Officer — a licensed physician responsible for receiving and reviewing laboratory results from drug tests and evaluating medical explanations for certain results (49 CFR §40.121). |
| **C/TPA** | Consortium/Third-Party Administrator — a service agent that provides or coordinates drug and alcohol testing services and may conduct Clearinghouse queries on behalf of employers (49 CFR §382.711). |
